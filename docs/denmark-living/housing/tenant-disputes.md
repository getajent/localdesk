# Tenant Disputes and Resolution

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/bolig-og-flytning/lejer-udlejer-konflikter  
> **Applies to**: All Tenants and Landlords

## Overview

Danish **lejer-udlejer konflikter** (tenant-landlord disputes) are resolved through **structured procedures**, **mediation services**, and **specialized courts** that protect both **tenant rights** and **landlord interests**. Understanding dispute resolution processes helps ensure **fair treatment** and **timely resolution** of housing conflicts.

## Key Information

- **Who can use**: All tenants and landlords in rental relationships
- **When to use**: For unresolved disputes after direct negotiation attempts
- **Prerequisites**: Valid rental agreement, documentation of dispute
- **Processing time**: Varies from weeks (mediation) to months (court proceedings)

## Common Types of Disputes

### Rent-Related Disputes

**Rental payment conflicts**:
- **Rent increases** above legal limits
- **Illegal rent** charges and overcharging
- **Rent reduction** requests for poor conditions
- **Service charges** and additional fees disputes
- **Deposit deductions** and return issues

### Property Condition Disputes

**Maintenance and repair conflicts**:
- **Landlord failure** to maintain property
- **Tenant damage** responsibility and costs
- **Habitability issues** and health hazards
- **Renovation disruptions** and compensation
- **Energy efficiency** and utility problems

### Lease Agreement Disputes

**Contract interpretation conflicts**:
- **Lease terms** and condition interpretations
- **Subletting rights** and restrictions
- **Pet policies** and restrictions
- **Parking and** storage rights
- **House rules** enforcement and violations

### Eviction and Termination Disputes

**End of tenancy conflicts**:
- **Illegal eviction** attempts and procedures
- **Notice period** violations and requirements
- **Termination grounds** and justifications
- **Moving-out procedures** and timelines
- **Key return** and final inspections

## Initial Resolution Steps

### Direct Communication

**First step in dispute resolution**:
- **Written communication** with landlord/tenant
- **Clear documentation** of issues and requests
- **Reasonable timeframes** for response and action
- **Professional tone** and factual presentation
- **Keep copies** of all correspondence

### Documentation Gathering

**Evidence collection**:
- **Photographs** of property conditions and damages
- **Written records** of communications and agreements
- **Receipts and** invoices for repairs or improvements
- **Witness statements** from neighbors or visitors
- **Expert assessments** for technical issues

### Informal Mediation

**Voluntary dispute resolution**:
- **Mutual agreement** to seek compromise
- **Third-party mediator** (friend, family, professional)
- **Cost-effective** and time-efficient approach
- **Preserves relationship** when possible
- **Non-binding** unless formal agreement reached

## Formal Dispute Resolution

### Housing Court (Boligret)

**Specialized rental dispute court**:
- **Jurisdiction over** most tenant-landlord disputes
- **Faster procedures** than general courts
- **Specialized judges** with housing law expertise
- **Lower costs** than regular court proceedings
- **Binding decisions** with enforcement powers

### Rent Tribunal (Huslejenævn)

**Municipal rent dispute boards**:
- **Rent level** disputes and assessments
- **Rent increase** approvals and rejections
- **Service charge** disputes and allocations
- **Property condition** impact on rent
- **Local expertise** in rental market conditions

### Consumer Complaints Board

**For certain housing disputes**:
- **Service provider** disputes (utilities, maintenance)
- **Consumer protection** issues
- **Alternative to** court proceedings
- **Free service** for consumers
- **Binding decisions** on service providers

## Housing Court Procedures

### Filing a Case

**Starting court proceedings**:
- **Complete application** forms with case details
- **Pay court fees** (typically 500-2,000 DKK)
- **Submit evidence** and supporting documentation
- **Serve notice** to opposing party
- **Await court** scheduling and procedures

### Court Process

**Hearing and decision procedures**:
- **Preliminary hearing** for case management
- **Evidence presentation** by both parties
- **Witness testimony** and expert opinions
- **Legal arguments** and case law references
- **Judge decision** and written judgment

### Types of Remedies

**Court-ordered solutions**:
- **Rent adjustments** up or down
- **Repair orders** and maintenance requirements
- **Compensation payments** for damages or inconvenience
- **Eviction orders** or tenancy protection
- **Injunctive relief** for ongoing issues

### Appeal Process

**Challenging court decisions**:
- **Appeal deadline**: 4 weeks from judgment
- **Higher court** review (Landsret)
- **Additional costs** and legal representation
- **Limited grounds** for appeal (legal errors, new evidence)
- **Final appeal** to Supreme Court (rare)

## Rent Tribunal Procedures

### Jurisdiction and Scope

**What rent tribunals handle**:
- **Rent level** assessments and comparisons
- **Rent increase** notifications and approvals
- **Operating cost** allocations and disputes
- **Property improvement** rent increases
- **Rent reduction** requests for poor conditions

### Application Process

**Filing with rent tribunal**:
- **Municipal tribunal** in property location
- **Application forms** and required documentation
- **Filing fee** (typically 2,000-4,000 DKK, refundable if successful)
- **Evidence submission** and case preparation
- **Hearing scheduling** and procedures

### Tribunal Composition

**Board membership**:
- **Professional judge** as chairman
- **Tenant representative** from tenant organizations
- **Landlord representative** from property owner organizations
- **Local expertise** in rental market conditions
- **Balanced perspective** on disputes

### Decision Implementation

**Enforcing tribunal decisions**:
- **Binding decisions** on rent levels and increases
- **Rent adjustment** effective dates
- **Refund calculations** for overpaid rent
- **Future rent** setting and guidelines
- **Compliance monitoring** and enforcement
