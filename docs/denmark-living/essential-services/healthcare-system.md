# Healthcare System Overview

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/sundhed-og-sygdom  
> **Applies to**: All Residents

## Overview

Denmark's **sundhedssystem** (healthcare system) provides universal healthcare coverage to all residents through a tax-funded public system. The system emphasizes **primary care** through general practitioners (GPs), with referrals to specialized services when needed. Healthcare is largely **free at the point of use** for residents with proper registration.

## Key Information

- **Who is covered**: All residents with CPR number and health insurance card
- **When coverage starts**: After receiving yellow health insurance card
- **Prerequisites**: CPR number, address registration, health insurance card
- **Cost**: Most services free, some co-payments for specific treatments

## Healthcare Structure

### Primary Care Level

**General Practitioners (GPs)**
- **First point of contact** for all non-emergency health issues
- **Mandatory registration** with one GP in your area
- **Referral system** - GP must refer you to specialists
- **Preventive care** including health checks and vaccinations
- **Free consultations** with health insurance card

### Secondary Care Level

**Hospitals and Specialists**
- **Public hospitals** provide free treatment
- **Specialist consultations** require GP referral
- **Emergency departments** available 24/7
- **Planned surgeries** and treatments
- **Outpatient clinics** for ongoing care

### Emergency Services

**Emergency Numbers**
- **112**: Life-threatening emergencies (ambulance, fire, police)
- **1813**: Medical helpline for urgent but non-life-threatening issues
- **Available 24/7** with English-speaking operators

## Health Insurance and Registration

### Yellow Health Insurance Card

**Sygesikringskort** (health insurance card) provides:
- **Free GP visits** and basic medical care
- **Free hospital treatment** in public system
- **Subsidized medications** through prescription system
- **Access to emergency services**

### Registration Process

1. **Obtain CPR number** and register address
2. **Receive yellow card** automatically by mail (2-3 weeks)
3. **Choose and register** with local GP
4. **Activate card** through first GP visit or online

### Group Classifications

**Group 1** (Most residents):
- **Free choice** of GP within your area
- **Free GP visits** and referrals
- **Standard coverage** for all basic services

**Group 2** (Optional):
- **Extended choice** of specialists without GP referral
- **Higher co-payments** for some services
- **Private insurance** often supplements this option

## Accessing Healthcare Services

### Non-Emergency Care

1. **Contact your GP** for appointment
2. **Describe symptoms** and urgency
3. **Attend appointment** with health insurance card
4. **Follow treatment plan** or referral recommendations

### Urgent Care

**1813 Medical Helpline**:
- **24/7 telephone consultation** with healthcare professionals
- **Triage service** determines appropriate care level
- **Advice and guidance** for non-emergency situations
- **Referrals** to emergency departments when necessary

### Emergency Care

**Emergency Departments**:
- **Life-threatening situations** only
- **Bring health insurance card** and identification
- **Triage system** prioritizes patients by severity
- **Free treatment** for residents

## Specialized Services

### Mental Health Services

**Psychiatric care**:
- **GP referral required** for specialist treatment
- **Crisis intervention** available through emergency services
- **Counseling services** available through municipalities
- **Private psychologists** (partial reimbursement with referral)

- **Not fully covered** by public health insurance
- **Subsidies available** for children and certain treatments
- **Private dental insurance** recommended for adults
- **Emergency dental care** available

### Maternity and Child Health

**Pregnancy and childbirth**:
- **Free prenatal care** through GP and hospitals
- **Free delivery** in public hospitals
- **Midwife services** included in coverage
- **Postnatal care** and support services

**Child healthcare**:
- **Free healthcare** for children under 18
- **Regular health checks** through municipal health nurses
- **School health services** and vaccinations
- **Specialized pediatric care** when needed

## Prescription Medications

### Pharmacy System

**Apotek** (pharmacy) services:
- **Prescription medications** subsidized by government
- **Over-the-counter** medications available without prescription
- **Medication reviews** and counseling services
- **Home delivery** available from some pharmacies

### Medication Costs

**Subsidy system**:
- **Graduated co-payments** based on annual medication costs
- **Maximum annual payment** cap for individuals
- **Free medications** for children and certain conditions
- **Chronic disease** support programs

## Private Healthcare Options

### Private Insurance

**Supplementary insurance** can provide:
- **Faster access** to specialists and procedures
- **Private hospital rooms** and amenities
- **Extended dental coverage**
- **Alternative treatments** not covered by public system

### Private Clinics

- **Direct access** without GP referral
- **Shorter waiting times** for non-urgent procedures
- **Full payment required** unless covered by private insurance
- **Quality standards** regulated by health authorities

## Healthcare for Specific Groups

### EU Citizens

- **European Health Insurance Card** provides temporary coverage
- **Same rights** as Danish residents once registered
- **Reciprocal agreements** with other EU countries
- **Travel coverage** within EU/EEA

### Non-EU Citizens

- **Full coverage** once properly registered with CPR
- **Travel insurance** recommended for trips outside Denmark
- **Residence permit** requirements may affect coverage
- **Integration support** available through municipalities

### Students

- **Full healthcare rights** with student residence permits
- **University health services** supplement public system
- **Mental health support** specifically for students
- **Sports medicine** and injury prevention services

## Common Health Issues and Resources

### Seasonal Considerations

**Winter health**:
- **Vitamin D deficiency** common due to limited sunlight
- **Seasonal depression** support services available
- **Flu vaccinations** recommended and often free

**Summer health**:
- **Tick-borne diseases** prevention and treatment
- **Sun safety** and skin cancer prevention
- **Allergy season** support and medications

### Preventive Care

**Health screenings**:
- **Cancer screening** programs (breast, cervical, colorectal)
- **Cardiovascular** risk assessments
- **Diabetes** prevention and management
- **Vaccination** programs for all ages

## Navigating the System

### Language Support

- **Interpreter services** available for medical appointments
- **Multilingual staff** at major hospitals
- **Translation services** for important medical documents
- **Cultural mediators** for complex cases

### Patient Rights

**Your rights include**:
- **Free treatment** in public system
- **Choice of hospital** for planned treatments
- **Second opinions** when requested
- **Access to medical records**
- **Complaint procedures** if dissatisfied with care

### Getting Help

**Support resources**:
- **Patient counselors** at hospitals
- **Municipal health services** for guidance
- **Patient organizations** for specific conditions
- **Online resources** and health portals
