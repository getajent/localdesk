# Transportation System

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/transport-og-trafik  
> **Applies to**: All Residents

## Overview

Denmark's **transportsystem** (transportation system) provides comprehensive mobility options through **public transit**, **cycling infrastructure**, and **private vehicle** networks. The system emphasizes **sustainability**, **accessibility**, and **integration** between different transport modes, with **cycling** playing a central role in daily transportation.

## Key Information

- **Who can use**: All residents and visitors
- **When available**: Public transit operates daily with varying schedules
- **Prerequisites**: Valid tickets/passes for public transit, registration for vehicles
- **Cost**: Varies by mode and distance, monthly passes available

## Public Transportation

### Regional and National Rail

**DSB (Danish State Railways)**
- **Intercity trains** connecting major cities
- **Regional trains** serving smaller towns
- **S-train network** in Copenhagen area
- **Integrated ticketing** with local transit

**Train types**:
- **InterCity (IC)**: Fast connections between major cities
- **Regional (RE)**: Slower trains with more stops
- **S-tog**: Suburban trains in Greater Copenhagen
- **Øresundståg**: Cross-border service to Sweden

### Metro Systems

**Copenhagen Metro**
- **Automated trains** operating 24/7
- **Four lines** (M1, M2, M3, M4) covering city and suburbs
- **Frequent service** every 2-6 minutes during peak hours
- **Integrated with** other public transport

**Aarhus Letbane** (Light Rail)
- **Modern light rail** system serving Aarhus region
- **Two lines** connecting city center with suburbs
- **Integration with** bus network
- **Frequent service** throughout the day

### Bus Networks

**Local bus services**:
- **Comprehensive coverage** in all cities and towns
- **Regular schedules** with real-time information
- **Accessibility features** for disabled passengers
- **Night bus services** in major cities

**Regional buses**:
- **Connections between** cities and rural areas
- **Coordination with** train schedules
- **Express services** on major routes
- **Tourist routes** to popular destinations

## Cycling Infrastructure

### Bike Lane Network

**Extensive cycling infrastructure**:
- **Dedicated bike lanes** on most major roads
- **Protected intersections** with traffic light priority
- **Bike highways** for longer commutes
- **Integration with** public transport (bike parking at stations)

### Bike Sharing Systems

**City bike programs**:
- **Bycyklen** (Copenhagen): Electric bikes with GPS and tablet
- **Aarhus City Bike**: Traditional and electric options
- **Municipal programs** in smaller cities
- **Tourist-friendly** options with multiple languages

### Cycling Support

**Infrastructure and services**:
- **Bike repair stations** throughout cities
- **Covered bike parking** at transport hubs
- **Bike shops** and maintenance services
- **Cycling maps** and route planning apps

## Ticketing and Payment Systems

### Integrated Ticketing

**Rejsekort** (Travel Card)
- **Contactless payment** for all public transport
- **Automatic fare** calculation based on distance
- **Discounts for** frequent travelers
- **Valid across** Denmark's public transport network

**Mobile ticketing**:
- **DOT Billetter** app for local transport
- **DSB app** for train tickets
- **Real-time information** and journey planning
- **Digital season passes** and subscriptions

### Fare Structure

**Zone-based pricing**:
- **Distance-based fares** with zone boundaries
- **Peak and off-peak** pricing in some areas
- **Group discounts** and family tickets
- **Student and senior** reduced fares

**Typical costs** (Copenhagen area):
- **Single journey**: 24-60 DKK depending on zones
- **Monthly pass**: 375-1,200 DKK depending on zones
- **Annual pass**: Significant discounts for yearly commitment

## Private Vehicle Transportation

### Car Ownership

**Vehicle registration** requirements:
- **Danish license plates** within 30 days of residency
- **Mandatory insurance** and annual inspection
- **High registration taxes** (25-180% of vehicle value)
- **Environmental considerations** affect taxation

### Parking Systems

**Urban parking**:
- **Paid parking zones** in city centers
- **Mobile payment** apps (EasyPark, ParkMan)
- **Resident parking** permits for local areas
- **Park and ride** facilities at transport hubs

**Parking costs**:
- **City center**: 15-40 DKK per hour
- **Residential areas**: 5-15 DKK per hour
- **Monthly permits**: 500-2,000 DKK depending on location

### Car Sharing

**Shared mobility options**:
- **GreenMobility**: Electric car sharing in major cities
- **DriveNow/ShareNow**: Premium car sharing service
- **Peer-to-peer** sharing platforms
- **Corporate car** sharing programs

## Alternative Transportation

### Electric Mobility

**E-mobility infrastructure**:
- **Electric vehicle** charging stations throughout country
- **E-bike sharing** systems in cities
- **Electric scooters** (rental and private)
- **Government incentives** for electric vehicle adoption

### Water Transportation

**Ferry services**:
- **Island connections** to Bornholm, Faroe Islands
- **Harbor buses** in Copenhagen
- **Recreational boat** services
- **International ferries** to Germany, Sweden, Norway

### Air Transportation

**Domestic flights**:
- **Copenhagen Airport** (CPH) - main international hub
- **Billund Airport** (BLL) - Jutland's main airport
- **Aalborg Airport** (AAL) - Northern Jutland
- **Regional airports** serving smaller communities

## Accessibility and Special Needs

### Accessible Public Transport

**Disability accommodations**:
- **Low-floor buses** and trains
- **Wheelchair accessibility** at stations
- **Audio and visual** announcements
- **Priority seating** and assistance services

### Special Transport Services

**Flextur** (Flexible transport):
- **Door-to-door service** for mobility-impaired residents
- **Advance booking** required
- **Subsidized fares** for eligible users
- **Available in** most municipalities

## Regional Transportation

### Greater Copenhagen Area

**Integrated transport network**:
- **S-train, metro, buses** with unified ticketing
- **Øresund Bridge** connection to Sweden
- **Comprehensive coverage** of metropolitan area
- **24/7 service** on key routes

### Jutland Peninsula

**Regional connections**:
- **IC trains** along major corridors
- **Regional buses** serving smaller towns
- **Ferry connections** to islands
- **Car-dependent** areas in rural regions

### Islands and Remote Areas

**Specialized services**:
- **Ferry schedules** coordinated with land transport
- **Subsidized transport** to maintain connectivity
- **Seasonal variations** in service levels
- **Tourist transport** during peak seasons
