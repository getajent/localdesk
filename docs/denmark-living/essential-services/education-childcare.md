# Education and Childcare System

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/familie-og-boern/boernepasning-og-skole  
> **Applies to**: All Families with Children

## Overview

Denmark's **uddannelses- og børnepasningssystem** (education and childcare system) provides comprehensive support from early childhood through higher education. The system emphasizes **equal access**, **high quality**, and **integration** of care and learning. Most services are **heavily subsidized** or free for residents.

## Key Information

- **Who is eligible**: All children of residents with CPR numbers
- **When to apply**: As early as possible, waiting lists are common
- **Prerequisites**: Child's CPR number, parent employment/study documentation
- **Processing time**: Varies by municipality and availability

## Early Childhood Care (0-6 years)

### Childcare Options

**Vuggestue** (Nursery, 0-3 years)
- **Professional childcare** for infants and toddlers
- **Educational activities** and social development
- **Meals and care** throughout the day
- **Qualified staff** with early childhood education

**Børnehave** (Kindergarten, 3-6 years)
- **Pre-school education** and play-based learning
- **School readiness** preparation
- **Social skills** development
- **Creative and physical** activities

**Integreret institution** (Integrated institution, 0-6 years)
- **Combined nursery and kindergarten** in one facility
- **Continuity of care** as children grow
- **Age-mixed activities** and learning
- **Streamlined enrollment** process

### Alternative Childcare

**Dagpleje** (Family daycare)
- **Home-based care** with registered childminders
- **Smaller groups** and family-like environment
- **Flexible arrangements** possible
- **Municipal oversight** and quality standards

**Private børnehave** (Private kindergartens)
- **Alternative pedagogical** approaches (Montessori, Waldorf, etc.)
- **Higher fees** than municipal options
- **Specialized programs** and philosophies
- **Still regulated** by municipal standards

## Primary and Lower Secondary Education (6-16 years)

### Folkeskole (Public School)

**Mandatory education** from age 6-16:
- **Free tuition** and materials
- **Local school** assignment based on address
- **Comprehensive curriculum** including Danish, math, science, arts
- **Inclusive education** for children with special needs

**School structure**:
- **Børnehaveklasse** (Pre-school class, age 6)
- **1st-9th grade** (ages 7-16)
- **Optional 10th grade** for additional preparation

### School Choice Options

**Free school choice** within municipality:
- **Apply to preferred schools** during enrollment period
- **Transportation** may be required for distant schools
- **Waiting lists** for popular schools
- **Special programs** (language immersion, arts focus, etc.)

### Private Schools

**Friskoler** (Independent schools):
- **Alternative pedagogies** and educational approaches
- **Partial government funding** reduces fees
- **Religious or philosophical** foundations
- **Still meet national** curriculum requirements

**International schools**:
- **English or other language** instruction
- **International curricula** (IB, British, American)
- **Higher fees** with limited subsidies
- **Popular with expat** families

## Upper Secondary Education (16-19 years)

### Academic Programs

**Gymnasium** (Academic high school):
- **3-year program** preparing for university
- **STX** (general academic), **HHX** (business), **HTX** (technical)
- **High academic standards** and university preparation
- **Free tuition** for residents

### Vocational Education

**Erhvervsuddannelser** (Vocational training):
- **Practical skills** training combined with theory
- **Apprenticeship programs** with employers
- **Direct pathway** to employment
- **Over 100 different** vocational programs

## Higher Education

### Universities

**Public universities** offer:
- **Free tuition** for EU/EEA students
- **Research-based** education and programs
- **International programs** in English
- **Student support** services and housing

**Major universities**:
- **University of Copenhagen** (KU)
- **Aarhus University** (AU)
- **Technical University of Denmark** (DTU)
- **Copenhagen Business School** (CBS)

### University Colleges

**Professionshøjskoler** provide:
- **Professional bachelor** programs
- **Practice-oriented** education
- **Teacher training**, nursing, social work, engineering
- **Strong employment** connections

## Enrollment and Application Processes

### Childcare Enrollment

**Municipal application process**:
1. **Register child** with CPR number
2. **Apply online** through municipal portal
3. **Provide documentation** of employment/study
4. **Choose preferred** institutions (up to 3-5 options)
5. **Wait for placement** offer (can take months)

**Required documents**:
- **Child's CPR certificate**
- **Parent employment** contracts or study enrollment
- **Proof of address** and residency
- **Special needs** documentation (if applicable)

### School Enrollment

**Automatic enrollment**:
- **Children automatically** enrolled in local folkeskole
- **Enrollment notification** sent by municipality
- **School assignment** based on residential address
- **Option to request** different school

**Private school applications**:
- **Direct application** to individual schools
- **Earlier deadlines** than public schools
- **Entrance requirements** may apply
- **Waiting lists** common for popular schools

## Costs and Financial Support

### Childcare Costs

**Municipal childcare fees**:
- **Income-based pricing** with maximum caps
- **Sibling discounts** for multiple children
- **Free childcare** for low-income families
- **Additional costs** for meals and activities

**Typical monthly costs** (varies by municipality):
- **Vuggestue**: 2,000-4,000 DKK
- **Børnehave**: 1,500-3,500 DKK
- **Dagpleje**: 1,800-3,800 DKK

### Education Costs

**Public education**:
- **Free tuition** through university level
- **Free textbooks** and basic materials
- **Transportation costs** for distant schools
- **Optional activities** may have fees

**Private education**:
- **Partial subsidies** reduce fees significantly
- **Monthly fees** typically 1,000-5,000 DKK
- **Additional costs** for materials and activities
- **Financial aid** available for qualifying families

## Special Needs and Support Services

### Special Education

**Inclusive education** approach:
- **Support in regular** classrooms when possible
- **Specialized schools** for severe needs
- **Individual education** plans (IEP)
- **Professional support** staff and resources

### Language Support

**For non-Danish speaking children**:
- **Danish as Second Language** (DSL) instruction
- **Bilingual support** teachers
- **Mother tongue** instruction available
- **Integration support** and cultural mediation

### Psychological and Social Support

**Student support services**:
- **School psychologists** and counselors
- **Social workers** for family support
- **Learning disabilities** assessment and support
- **Behavioral intervention** programs

## After-School and Holiday Care

### SFO (School-Age Childcare)

**Before and after school care**:
- **Available at most** folkeskoler
- **Homework help** and recreational activities
- **Holiday programs** during school breaks
- **Additional fees** apply

### Youth Programs

**Fritidsklub** (Youth clubs):
- **After-school activities** for older children
- **Sports, arts, and** social programs
- **Peer interaction** and skill development
- **Municipal or private** operation

## International Families

### School Integration

**Support for expat families**:
- **International welcome** classes
- **Cultural orientation** programs
- **Parent information** sessions in multiple languages
- **Buddy systems** for new students

### Maintaining Heritage

**Heritage language** support:
- **Mother tongue** instruction available
- **Cultural associations** and community schools
- **International curriculum** options
- **Dual language** development programs

## Parent Involvement

### School Participation

**Parent engagement** opportunities:
- **School boards** with parent representatives
- **Parent-teacher** conferences and communication
- **Volunteer opportunities** and school events
- **Parent associations** and support groups

### Rights and Responsibilities

**Parent rights** include:
- **Choice of school** within reasonable limits
- **Information access** about child's progress
- **Participation in** educational decisions
- **Complaint procedures** for disputes
