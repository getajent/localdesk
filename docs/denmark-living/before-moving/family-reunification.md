# Family Reunification Procedures

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://www.nyidanmark.dk/en-GB/You-want-to-apply/Family  
> **Applies to**: Families Seeking to Reunite in Denmark

## Overview

Danish **familiesammenføring** (family reunification) allows **family members** to join their relatives who are **legally residing** in Denmark. The process involves **strict requirements**, **documentation procedures**, and **integration obligations** designed to ensure **successful settlement** and **social cohesion** while respecting **family unity** rights.

## Key Information

- **Who can apply**: Spouses, children, and other qualifying family members
- **When to apply**: After sponsor establishes legal residence in Denmark
- **Prerequisites**: Sponsor eligibility, relationship documentation, financial requirements
- **Processing time**: 6-12 months for most applications

## Types of Family Reunification

### Spouse and Partner Reunification

**Marriage and partnership-based reunification**:
- **Married spouses** with legal marriage documentation
- **Registered partnerships** (same-sex and opposite-sex)
- **Cohabiting partners** with documented relationship (limited circumstances)
- **Divorced spouses** with shared custody of children
- **Widowed spouses** in specific circumstances

### Child Reunification

**Minor children joining parents**:
- **Biological children** under 18 years old
- **Adopted children** with legal adoption documentation
- **Stepchildren** in certain circumstances
- **Foster children** with legal guardianship
- **Adult children** with disabilities requiring care

### Parent and Grandparent Reunification

**Elderly family member reunification**:
- **Parents over 60** requiring care and support
- **Grandparents** in exceptional circumstances
- **Dependency requirements** and care needs documentation
- **Limited approval** rates and strict criteria
- **Alternative support** options must be unavailable

### Other Family Members

**Extended family reunification**:
- **Siblings** in exceptional humanitarian circumstances
- **Other relatives** with special dependency relationships
- **Unaccompanied minors** with family connections
- **Humanitarian cases** with compelling circumstances
- **Very limited** approval rates

## Sponsor Requirements

### Legal Status Requirements

**Sponsor eligibility criteria**:
- **Danish citizenship** or permanent residence permit
- **EU/EEA citizens** with residence registration
- **Temporary residence** permit holders (limited circumstances)
- **Refugee status** or subsidiary protection
- **Continuous legal** residence in Denmark

### Financial Requirements

**Economic support obligations**:
- **Minimum income**: 270,000 DKK annually (2025, adjusted yearly)
- **Stable employment** or business income
- **No public benefits** received in past 3 years (with exceptions)
- **Financial guarantee**: Deposit of 100,000 DKK
- **Housing standards**: Adequate accommodation for family size

### Integration Requirements

**Sponsor integration criteria**:
- **No serious** criminal convictions
- **Danish language** skills (varies by sponsor status)
- **Integration assessment** and community ties
- **Compliance with** Danish laws and values
- **Active participation** in Danish society

### Housing Requirements

**Accommodation standards**:
- **Minimum space**: 20m² for couple, 10m² per additional person
- **Separate bedrooms** for children over 6 of different genders
- **Basic facilities**: Kitchen, bathroom, heating
- **Legal occupancy** and rental agreements
- **Housing inspection** may be required

## Application Process

### Documentation Requirements

**Required documents for application**:
- **Relationship proof**: Marriage certificates, birth certificates
- **Identity documents**: Passports, national ID cards
- **Financial documentation**: Employment contracts, tax returns, bank statements
- **Housing documentation**: Rental agreements, property deeds, housing inspection
- **Criminal record** checks from all countries of residence
- **Medical examinations** and health certificates

### Application Submission

**Filing procedures**:
1. **Online application** through New to Denmark portal
2. **Document upload** and verification
3. **Application fee** payment (varies by case type)
4. **Biometric data** collection at Danish mission abroad
5. **Interview scheduling** if required
6. **Additional documentation** requests if needed

### Processing Timeline

**Application review stages**:
- **Initial review**: 2-4 weeks for completeness check
- **Substantive assessment**: 4-8 months for decision
- **Additional information**: May extend processing time
- **Appeal process**: Additional 6-12 months if appealed
- **Visa issuance**: 2-4 weeks after approval

## Specific Requirements by Category

### Spouse Reunification

**Marriage-based applications**:
- **Minimum age**: Both spouses must be 24 years old
- **Genuine relationship**: Evidence of authentic marriage
- **Shared residence**: Intention to live together in Denmark
- **Language requirements**: Danish language test for some applicants
- **Integration contract**: Agreement to participate in integration programs

### Child Reunification

**Minor children applications**:
- **Custody rights**: Legal custody or consent from other parent
- **Best interests**: Child welfare assessment
- **Educational needs**: School enrollment and language support
- **Healthcare**: Medical examination and vaccination records
- **Age verification**: Documentation of child's age and identity

### Refugee Family Reunification

**Special procedures for refugees**:
- **Expedited processing**: Faster timeline for refugee families
- **Reduced requirements**: Lower financial thresholds
- **Extended family**: Broader definition of eligible family members
- **Humanitarian considerations**: Special circumstances evaluation
- **Integration support**: Enhanced settlement assistance

## Financial Obligations and Support

### Financial Guarantee (Sikkerhedsstillelse)

**Deposit requirements**:
- **Amount**: 100,000 DKK deposit required
- **Purpose**: Covers potential public benefits costs
- **Duration**: Held for 7 years or until citizenship
- **Return conditions**: No public benefits received
- **Interest**: Deposit earns interest during holding period

### Maintenance Obligation

**Ongoing financial responsibility**:
- **Support duration**: Until family member becomes self-sufficient
- **Scope**: Housing, food, healthcare, education costs
- **Public benefits**: Sponsor liable for benefits received
- **Legal enforcement**: Court-ordered support if necessary
- **Insurance options**: Private insurance to cover obligations

### Income Documentation

**Proving financial capacity**:
- **Employment contracts** and salary statements
- **Tax returns** for previous 3 years
- **Bank statements** showing stable income
- **Business documentation** for self-employed sponsors
- **Pension statements** for retired sponsors

## Integration Requirements and Support

### Danish Language Learning

**Language obligations**:
- **Mandatory courses** for adult family members
- **Language tests** and proficiency requirements
- **Free tuition** for eligible participants
- **Childcare support** during language classes
- **Flexible scheduling** for working family members

### Integration Contract

**Settlement agreement obligations**:
- **Program participation**: Active engagement in integration activities
- **Employment goals**: Job search and skills development
- **Community involvement**: Participation in Danish society
- **Progress monitoring**: Regular assessment and support
- **Consequences**: Potential permit implications for non-compliance

### Cultural Orientation

**Danish society introduction**:
- **Values and norms**: Understanding Danish culture and expectations
- **Legal system**: Rights and obligations under Danish law
- **Social services**: Access to healthcare, education, and support
- **Employment market**: Job search skills and workplace culture
- **Civic participation**: Democratic processes and community involvement

## Rights and Obligations

### Family Member Rights

**Entitlements after arrival**:
- **Residence permit**: Legal right to live in Denmark
- **Work authorization**: Right to employment (may have initial restrictions)
- **Healthcare access**: Coverage under Danish health system
- **Education**: Access to schools and educational programs
- **Social services**: Eligibility for certain benefits and support

### Sponsor Obligations

**Ongoing responsibilities**:
- **Financial support**: Maintenance and housing provision
- **Integration assistance**: Support for language learning and settlement
- **Legal compliance**: Ensuring family member follows Danish laws
- **Reporting changes**: Notification of address, employment, or relationship changes
- **Permit renewal**: Assistance with residence permit extensions

### Mutual Obligations

**Shared responsibilities**:
- **Cohabitation**: Living together as intended (for spouses)
- **Relationship maintenance**: Genuine family relationships
- **Integration participation**: Active engagement in Danish society
- **Legal compliance**: Following all Danish laws and regulations
- **Communication**: Cooperation with authorities when required

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Documentation Issues

**Frequent problems and solutions**:
- **Missing documents**: Obtain certified copies and translations
- **Document authenticity**: Use official translation services
- **Foreign document** recognition: Apostille or embassy certification
- **Language barriers**: Professional translation and interpretation
- **Document updates**: Keep all documentation current

### Financial Requirements

**Meeting economic criteria**:
- **Income improvement**: Career development and additional employment
- **Alternative sponsors**: Other eligible family members as sponsors
- **Financial planning**: Budgeting and financial management assistance
- **Legal advice**: Professional guidance on requirements
- **Appeal options**: Challenging negative decisions

### Integration Challenges

**Settlement difficulties**:
- **Language barriers**: Intensive language study and practice
- **Cultural adaptation**: Cultural orientation and community support
- **Employment challenges**: Skills recognition and job search assistance
- **Social isolation**: Community connections and support networks
- **Children's adjustment**: School support and counseling services

## Appeal and Review Process

### Grounds for Appeal

**Basis for challenging decisions**:
- **Legal errors**: Incorrect application of immigration law
- **Factual mistakes**: Errors in case assessment or documentation
- **Procedural violations**: Improper handling of application
- **New evidence**: Additional documentation or changed circumstances
- **Humanitarian considerations**: Compelling personal circumstances

### Appeal Procedures

**Challenging negative decisions**:
1. **Appeal deadline**: 8 weeks from decision notification
2. **Appeal submission**: Written appeal with supporting arguments
3. **Legal representation**: Professional assistance recommended
4. **Review process**: Independent assessment by appeals board
5. **Decision timeline**: 6-12 months for appeal resolution

### Alternative Options

**Other remedies and approaches**:
- **Reapplication**: New application with improved documentation
- **Humanitarian applications**: Special circumstances consideration
- **EU law rights**: Alternative legal basis for family unity
- **Temporary permits**: Short-term solutions while resolving issues
- **Legal action**: Court proceedings in exceptional cases
