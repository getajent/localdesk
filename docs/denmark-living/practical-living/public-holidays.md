# Public Holidays and Seasonal Considerations

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/kultur-og-fritid/helligdage-og-hojtider  
> **Applies to**: All Residents and Visitors

## Overview

Denmark's **helligdage og højtider** (public holidays and celebrations) reflect the country's **Christian heritage**, **cultural traditions**, and **modern secular celebrations**. Understanding these holidays helps with **work planning**, **service availability**, and **cultural integration** into Danish society.

## Key Information

- **Who is affected**: All residents, workers, and businesses in Denmark
- **When holidays occur**: Fixed dates and moveable holidays throughout the year
- **Prerequisites**: None - holidays are observed nationally
- **Impact**: Reduced services, business closures, and cultural celebrations

## Official Public Holidays

### New Year's Day (Nytårsdag)

**January 1**
- **National holiday** with most businesses closed
- **Public transportation** reduced schedules
- **Family gatherings** and recovery from New Year's Eve
- **Traditional foods**: Kransekage and champagne
- **Fireworks displays** and celebrations continue from previous evening

### Maundy Thursday (Skærtorsdag)

**Thursday before Easter**
- **Religious significance**: Commemorates Last Supper
- **Half-day holiday** - many businesses close early
- **Traditional meal**: Green soup (grønkålssuppe) and lamb
- **Church services** and religious observances
- **Beginning of** Easter weekend

### Good Friday (Langfredag)

**Friday before Easter**
- **Full public holiday** with most services closed
- **Solemn religious** observance day
- **No alcohol sales** in some municipalities
- **Quiet day** with limited entertainment and activities
- **Traditional foods**: Fish and simple meals

### Easter Sunday (Påskedag)

**First Sunday after first full moon following spring equinox**
- **Major Christian** celebration
- **Family gatherings** and traditional meals
- **Easter eggs** and chocolate traditions
- **Church services** and religious celebrations
- **Spring cleaning** and renewal themes

### Easter Monday (2. Påskedag)

**Monday after Easter Sunday**
- **Extended Easter** weekend holiday
- **Family time** and leisure activities
- **Traditional games** and outdoor activities
- **Many businesses** remain closed
- **End of Easter** holiday period

### Great Prayer Day (Store Bededag)

**Fourth Friday after Easter**
- **Unique Danish** Protestant holiday
- **Religious reflection** and prayer day
- **Traditional food**: Warm wheat buns (varme hveder)
- **Church services** and community gatherings
- **Spring celebration** and renewal

### Ascension Day (Kristi Himmelfartsdag)

**39 days after Easter**
- **Christian holiday** commemorating Jesus's ascension
- **Popular for** outdoor activities and trips
- **Father's Day** celebration in Denmark
- **Many take** extended weekend (bridge day Friday)
- **Good weather** often coincides with holiday

### Whit Sunday (Pinsedag)

**49 days after Easter**
- **Pentecost celebration** in Christian calendar
- **Family gatherings** and outdoor activities
- **Traditional beginning** of summer season
- **Confirmation ceremonies** often held
- **Spring festivals** and community events

### Whit Monday (2. Pinsedag)

**Monday after Whit Sunday**
- **Extended Pentecost** weekend
- **Popular travel** and vacation time
- **Outdoor activities** and nature trips
- **Many businesses** closed for long weekend
- **Bridge to** summer vacation season

### Constitution Day (Grundlovsdag)

**June 5**
- **Celebrates Danish** constitution (1849)
- **Half-day holiday** - businesses often close early
- **Political speeches** and democratic celebrations
- **Folk high school** graduations and ceremonies
- **Patriotic displays** and flag flying

### Christmas Eve (Juleaftensdag)

**December 24**
- **Most important** holiday in Denmark
- **Businesses close** early (usually by noon)
- **Family gatherings** and traditional dinner
- **Gift exchange** and Christmas tree celebration
- **Church services** in evening

### Christmas Day (Juledag)

**December 25**
- **Full public holiday** with everything closed
- **Continued family** celebrations
- **Traditional foods** and leftovers
- **Quiet day** for rest and reflection
- **Religious services** and observances

### Boxing Day (2. Juledag)

**December 26**
- **Extended Christmas** celebration
- **Family visits** and social gatherings
- **Shopping and** post-Christmas activities
- **Sports events** and entertainment
- **Preparation for** New Year celebrations

## Moveable Holidays

### Easter Calculation

**Determining Easter dates**:
- **Based on lunar** calendar and spring equinox
- **Varies each year** between March 22 and April 25
- **Affects multiple** holidays (Maundy Thursday through Whit Monday)
- **Long-term planning** requires calendar consultation
- **Orthodox Easter** may differ from Western Easter

### Bridge Days (Brofri)

**Extended weekends**:
- **Taking Friday** off when holiday falls on Thursday
- **Taking Monday** off when holiday falls on Tuesday
- **Popular practice** for longer vacations
- **Employer policies** vary on bridge day approval
- **Planning ahead** recommended for popular bridge days

## Seasonal Celebrations

### Winter Season

**December - February**
- **Lucia Day** (December 13) - Swedish tradition adopted
- **New Year's Eve** (December 31) - major celebration
- **Fastelavn** (February/March) - Danish carnival before Lent
- **Dark season** activities and hygge culture
- **Winter sports** and indoor entertainment

### Spring Season

**March - May**
- **Easter celebrations** and religious observances
- **May Day** (May 1) - labor movement celebrations
- **Spring cleaning** and renewal activities
- **Outdoor activities** resume as weather improves
- **Confirmation season** and family celebrations

### Summer Season

**June - August**
- **Midsummer** (June 23) - Sankt Hans Aften bonfires
- **Summer vacation** period (July-August)
- **Outdoor festivals** and cultural events
- **Long daylight** hours and outdoor living
- **Beach and** nature activities

### Autumn Season

**September - November**
- **Back to school** and work routines
- **Harvest festivals** and seasonal foods
- **Cultural season** begins (theater, concerts)
- **Preparation for** winter and holiday season
- **Cozy indoor** activities and hygge

### Retail and Shopping

**Holiday shopping patterns**:
- **Extended hours** before major holidays
- **Complete closure** on most public holidays
- **Limited Sunday** shopping throughout year
- **Holiday sales** and seasonal promotions
- **Tourist areas** may have different schedules

### Public Transportation

**Holiday service schedules**:
- **Reduced frequency** on public holidays
- **Sunday schedules** often used on holidays
- **Special event** transportation for celebrations
- **Advanced planning** recommended for holiday travel
- **International connections** may be affected

### Healthcare Services

**Medical care during holidays**:
- **Emergency services** always available
- **Reduced clinic** hours and appointments
- **Pharmacy schedules** limited on holidays
- **Dental and** specialist care often closed
- **Plan ahead** for prescription refills

### Government Services

**Public administration during holidays**:
- **All government** offices closed on public holidays
- **Digital services** available 24/7
- **Passport and** visa services suspended
- **Court proceedings** and legal services closed
- **Emergency services** maintain operations

- **Christmas**: Roast duck/pork, red cabbage, potatoes
- **Easter**: Lamb, eggs, chocolate
- **Midsummer**: Strawberries and cream

**Present exchange traditions**:
