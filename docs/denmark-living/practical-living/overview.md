## Quick Tips

### Shopping
- **Discount chains**: Netto, Rema 1000, Fakta (cheapest)
- **Mid-range**: Føtex, SuperBrugsen, Kvickly
- **Discount apps**: Too Good To Go (food waste), eTilbudsavis (weekly deals)
- **Shopping hours**: Most close by 20:00-22:00, limited Sunday hours

### Cultural Notes
- **Punctuality**: Highly valued, arrive on time
- **Direct communication**: Danes are straightforward and honest
- **Personal space**: Respect privacy and personal boundaries
- **Hygge**: Cozy, comfortable atmosphere - important cultural concept
- **Work-life balance**: Highly valued, leave work at work

### Money-Saving
- **Second-hand**: Genbrugsbutikker (thrift stores), DBA.dk, Facebook Marketplace
- **Libraries**: Free books, movies, music, and activities
- **Cycling**: Cheapest transportation, excellent infrastructure
- **Meal planning**: Reduce food waste, shop with list
- **Happy hours**: Many restaurants offer deals 17:00-19:00

### Transportation
- **Cycling**: Primary mode for many Danes
- **Public transit**: Efficient but can be expensive
- **Rejsekort**: Travel card with discounts
- **DOT tickets**: Mobile app for tickets
- **Bike lanes**: Extensive network, follow rules carefully

### Social Integration
- **Language**: Learn Danish - essential for integration
- **Foreninger**: Join clubs/associations (sports, hobbies, etc.)
- **Workplace**: Good place to make friends
- **Expat groups**: International Community, InterNations, Facebook groups
- **Volunteer**: Great way to meet people and practice Danish

## Seasonal Considerations

### Winter (December-February)
- **Dark**: Limited daylight (8 hours or less)
- **Cold**: 0-5°C, dress warmly
- **Hygge season**: Cozy indoor activities
- **Christmas**: Major holiday, most things closed Dec 24-26

### Spring (March-May)
- **Brightening**: Increasing daylight
- **Mild**: 5-15°C
- **Outdoor activities**: Parks and outdoor spaces come alive
- **Easter**: Long weekend, many businesses closed

### Summer (June-August)
- **Long days**: Up to 17 hours of daylight
- **Warm**: 15-25°C (occasionally warmer)
- **Vacation season**: Many Danes away in July
- **Outdoor living**: Beaches, parks, outdoor dining

### Autumn (September-November)
- **Cooling**: 10-15°C
- **Darkening**: Rapidly decreasing daylight
- **Cozy season**: Indoor activities increase
- **Back to routine**: After summer holidays

## Common Questions

### Is Denmark expensive?

Yes, compared to many countries. High costs for:
- Housing (especially Copenhagen)
- Dining out
- Alcohol
- Transportation

More affordable:
- Healthcare (free/subsidized)
- Education (free)
- Childcare (subsidized)
- Public services

### Do I need to speak Danish?

For daily life: Not essential, most Danes speak excellent English
For integration: Yes, highly recommended
For work: Depends on field, but Danish helps significantly
For citizenship: Required (Danish language test)

### How do I make friends?

- Join foreninger (clubs/associations)
- Take Danish classes (meet other newcomers)
- Workplace connections
- Expat groups and events
- Volunteer activities
- Sports and hobbies

### What should I know about cycling?

- **Rules**: Follow traffic laws, use bike lanes
- **Lights**: Required in dark (fines for missing lights)
- **Hand signals**: Indicate turns
- **Right of way**: Bikes have priority in bike lanes
- **Theft**: Lock your bike securely (two locks recommended)

### What's the deal with tipping?

- **Restaurants**: Service included, tipping optional (round up or 5-10%)
- **Taxis**: Round up fare
- **Delivery**: Not expected
- **Bars**: Not expected
- Generally: Tipping not mandatory, wages are fair
