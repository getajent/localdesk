## Overview

Denmark is known for its high cost of living, but there are numerous strategies and resources to help you save money on everyday expenses. This guide covers discount apps, second-hand markets, budget-friendly shopping options, and practical money-saving tips for housing, transportation, food, and entertainment. By leveraging these resources and adopting smart spending habits, you can significantly reduce your living costs in Denmark.

## Key Information

- **Who needs this**: All residents, especially students and newcomers on a budget
- **Potential savings**: 20-40% on monthly expenses with strategic planning
- **Essential apps**: Too Good To Go, MobilePay, Nemtilbud, DBA
- **Best resources**: Second-hand markets, discount stores, library services

## Discount Apps and Digital Tools

### Too Good To Go

**Too Good To Go** is Denmark's most popular app for saving food from waste and saving money:
- Purchase surplus food from restaurants, bakeries, and supermarkets at 50-70% discount
- "Magic bags" contain surprise items that would otherwise be thrown away
- Typical price: 30-40 DKK for food worth 100+ DKK
- Pick up at specified times (usually end of day)
- Environmental benefit: reduce food waste while saving money
- Download: [toogoodtogo.dk](https://toogoodtogo.dk/)

### Nemtilbud (Discount Offers)

**Nemtilbud** aggregates weekly offers from all Danish supermarkets:
- Browse all supermarket deals in one place
- Create shopping lists based on current offers
- Compare prices across stores
- Plan meals around discounted items
- Free app available for iOS and Android

### eTilbudsavis (Digital Flyers)

**eTilbudsavis** provides digital versions of all store flyers:
- View weekly offers from supermarkets, electronics stores, and more
- Search for specific products on sale
- Set alerts for items you want
- Reduce paper waste by going digital
- Free app and website

### MobilePay Deals

**MobilePay** offers exclusive deals and cashback:
- Special offers at participating stores and restaurants
- Cashback on purchases
- Split bills easily with friends
- Most widely used payment app in Denmark
- Essential for everyday transactions

### Reshopper

**Reshopper** helps you find the best prices on groceries:
- Compare prices across different supermarkets
- Find the cheapest store for your shopping list
- Track price history
- Discover which stores have the best deals on specific items

## Second-Hand Shopping

### DBA (Den Blå Avis)

**DBA** is Denmark's largest online marketplace for second-hand items:
- Buy and sell furniture, electronics, clothing, and more
- Free to browse and post listings
- Local pickup reduces shipping costs
- Negotiate prices with sellers
- Categories include everything from apartments to baby items
- Website: [dba.dk](https://www.dba.dk/)

### Facebook Marketplace

**Facebook Marketplace** is popular for local second-hand sales:
- Browse items in your area
- Direct messaging with sellers
- Often free items available ("gratis" or "bortgives")
- Join local buy/sell groups for your city
- Good for furniture, household items, and clothing

### Genbrugsbutikker (Thrift Stores)

Denmark has many charity thrift stores:
- **Røde Kors** (Red Cross): Clothing, furniture, household items
- **Kirkens Korshær**: Furniture and household goods
- **Folkekirkens Nødhjælp**: Clothing and accessories
- **Emmaus**: Furniture, electronics, and miscellaneous items
- Prices are very affordable (10-50% of retail)
- Support charitable causes while saving money

### Loppemarkeder (Flea Markets)

Flea markets are popular weekend activities:
- Held regularly in most cities
- Find vintage items, furniture, clothing, and collectibles
- Bargaining is expected and encouraged
- Check local event listings for dates and locations
- Early arrival gets the best selection

### Trendsales and Resecond

Online platforms for second-hand fashion:
- **Trendsales**: Buy and sell clothing, shoes, and accessories
- **Resecond**: Curated second-hand designer items
- Lower prices than retail
- Sustainable fashion choices
- Easy returns and buyer protection

## Food and Grocery Savings

### Shop at Discount Supermarkets

Discount stores offer 20-30% savings compared to premium supermarkets:
- **Netto**, **Rema 1000**, **Aldi**, **Lidl**: Best prices on basics
- Store brands are 30-50% cheaper than name brands
- Weekly offers provide additional savings
- See [Shopping Guide](shopping-guide.md) for detailed store information

### Evening Discounts

Many supermarkets discount perishables in the evening:
- Look for yellow **nedsat** (reduced) stickers
- Discounts of 30-50% on items near expiration
- Best time: 1-2 hours before closing
- Bread, meat, and prepared foods are commonly discounted
- Freeze items if you can't use them immediately

### Buy in Bulk

Bulk buying saves money on non-perishables:
- **Bilka** and **Føtex** have bulk sections
- Split bulk purchases with friends or roommates
- Stock up during sales on items you use regularly
- Warehouse stores like **Bilka** offer larger package sizes at lower per-unit costs

### Meal Planning and Cooking

Strategic meal planning reduces food costs:
- Plan weekly menus around sale items
- Cook in batches and freeze portions
- Bring lunch from home (saves 50-80 DKK per day)
- Use leftovers creatively
- Avoid impulse purchases with a shopping list

### Bottle and Can Deposits

Return bottles and cans for **pant** (deposit refunds):
- Small bottles/cans: 1 DKK each
- Large bottles: 3 DKK each
- Use reverse vending machines (**pantautomater**) in supermarkets
- Can add up to 50-100 DKK per month for regular consumers

## Housing and Utilities Savings

### Housing Benefits (Boligstøtte)

If eligible, apply for **boligstøtte** (housing benefits):
- Government subsidy for rent costs
- Based on income, rent amount, and household size
- Can reduce rent by 1,000-3,000 DKK per month
- See [Boligstøtte guide](../social-benefits/boligstotte.md) for eligibility

### Share Accommodation

Sharing reduces housing costs significantly:
- **Kollegie** (student housing): Affordable shared facilities
- **Bofællesskab** (co-housing): Shared living spaces
- Find roommates on DBA, Facebook groups, or **findbolig.nu**
- Split rent, utilities, and internet costs

### Energy Savings

Reduce utility bills with energy-conscious habits:
- Turn off lights and electronics when not in use
- Use energy-efficient LED bulbs
- Lower heating by 1-2 degrees (saves 5-10% on heating)
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Air-dry laundry instead of using dryers
- Take shorter showers

### Compare Internet and Phone Plans

Shop around for the best deals:
- **Oister**, **Telmore**, **Lebara**: Budget mobile providers
- Prepaid plans are often cheaper than contracts
- Bundle internet and mobile for discounts
- Consider data-only plans if you use VoIP for calls

## Transportation Savings

### Bicycle as Primary Transport

Cycling is the cheapest transportation option:
- Buy a used bike on DBA (500-1,500 DKK)
- No fuel, insurance, or parking costs
- Faster than driving in city centers
- Health benefits as a bonus
- See [Cycling Culture guide](cycling-culture.md) for tips

### Public Transportation Discounts

Save on public transport with smart strategies:
- **Rejsekort**: Reloadable card with discounted fares (up to 20% off)
- **Monthly passes**: Cost-effective for daily commuters
- **Youth discounts**: Available for students under 26
- **Pendlerkort**: Commuter passes for regular routes
- **Bike + train**: Combine cycling with public transport

### Carpooling and Ride-Sharing

Share rides to reduce costs:
- **GoMore**: Carpooling platform for longer trips
- **BlaBlaCar**: International ride-sharing
- Split fuel costs with other passengers
- Good for weekend trips and intercity travel

### Avoid Car Ownership

Cars are expensive in Denmark:
- High registration tax (up to 150% of car value)
- Insurance, fuel, and parking costs
- Consider car-sharing services like **GreenMobility** or **DriveNow** for occasional needs
- Rent cars only when necessary

## Entertainment and Leisure Savings

### Free and Low-Cost Activities

Denmark offers many free entertainment options:
- **Public libraries**: Free books, movies, music, and internet access
- **Parks and beaches**: Free outdoor recreation
- **Museums**: Many have free admission days (check websites)
- **Community events**: Free concerts, festivals, and cultural events
- **Hiking and nature**: Denmark has extensive free nature areas

### Library Services

Danish libraries offer more than books:
- **Bibliotek.dk**: Access to books, e-books, audiobooks, and movies
- Free internet and computer access
- Language learning resources
- Cultural events and workshops
- Meeting spaces
- All services are free with a library card (requires CPR number)

### Student Discounts

If you're a student, take advantage of discounts:
- **Student ID**: Show at museums, cinemas, and attractions
- **SU card**: Provides additional discounts
- Many restaurants offer student lunch deals
- Public transport discounts for students under 26

### Fitness and Sports

Stay active without expensive gym memberships:
- **Outdoor gyms**: Free workout equipment in parks
- **Running and cycling**: Free exercise options
- **Community sports clubs**: Affordable membership fees
- **YouTube workouts**: Free home exercise videos
- **Swimming**: Public pools are affordable (30-50 DKK per visit)

## Clothing and Personal Care

### Second-Hand Clothing

Buy clothes second-hand for major savings:
- Thrift stores: 20-50 DKK for shirts, 50-150 DKK for jackets
- **Trendsales** and **Resecond**: Online second-hand fashion
- Flea markets: Bargain prices on vintage and used clothing
- Clothing swaps: Free exchanges with friends or community groups

### End-of-Season Sales

Time purchases for maximum discounts:
- **Udsalg** (sales): January and July have major sales
- Buy winter clothes in spring, summer clothes in fall
- Discounts of 50-70% on seasonal items
- Online shopping often has better deals than physical stores

### DIY Personal Care

Reduce personal care costs:
- Cut your own hair or find budget salons
- Use library resources for beauty and grooming tips
- Buy generic brands for toiletries
- Make your own cleaning products (vinegar, baking soda)

## Banking and Financial Tips

### Avoid ATM Fees

Minimize banking fees:
- Use your bank's ATMs to avoid fees
- Pay with card or MobilePay instead of cash
- Choose a bank with low or no monthly fees
- Consider online banks like **Lunar** or **Revolut** for lower fees

### Budget Tracking

Monitor spending to identify savings opportunities:
- Use banking apps to track expenses
- Set monthly budgets for categories
- Review spending regularly
- Identify areas where you can cut back
- Apps like **Spiir** help with budget management

### Take Advantage of Tax Deductions

Maximize tax deductions to reduce tax burden:
- Commuting costs (if over 24 km each way)
- Union and A-kasse membership fees
- Interest on loans
- See [Tax Deductions guide](../tax-finance/tax-deductions.md) for details

## Seasonal Money-Saving Strategies

### Summer Savings

- Free outdoor concerts and festivals
- Beach activities (free)
- Picnics instead of restaurant meals
- Camping for affordable vacations
- Farmers markets for fresh, affordable produce

### Winter Savings

- **Julefrokost** (Christmas lunch): Potluck style with friends instead of expensive restaurants
- Indoor activities at libraries and community centers
- Winter sales in January (up to 70% off)
- Stay warm at home to reduce heating costs elsewhere
- Hot drinks at home instead of cafes

## Common Questions

### How much can I realistically save with these tips?

By combining multiple strategies (discount stores, second-hand shopping, cycling, cooking at home), you can reduce monthly expenses by 2,000-5,000 DKK or more, depending on your current spending habits.

### Are second-hand items good quality in Denmark?

Yes, Danes generally take good care of their belongings, and second-hand items are often in excellent condition. Many people sell items they no longer need rather than damaged goods.

### Is it worth getting a Rejsekort?

Yes, if you use public transportation regularly. The Rejsekort offers automatic discounts and is more convenient than buying individual tickets. It pays for itself within a few weeks of regular use.

### Can I live comfortably on a student budget in Denmark?

Yes, with careful planning and use of these cost-saving strategies. Many students live comfortably on SU (student grants) combined with part-time work, especially if they share housing and cook at home.

### What are the best apps to download when moving to Denmark?

Essential money-saving apps: Too Good To Go, MobilePay, Nemtilbud, DBA, Rejseplanen (public transport), and your bank's app for expense tracking.
