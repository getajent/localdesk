# Workplace Rights and Safety

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/arbejde-dagpenge-og-sygedagpenge/arbejdsmiljoe-og-sikkerhed  
> **Applies to**: All Employees and Employers

## Overview

Danish **arbejdspladsrettigheder og sikkerhed** (workplace rights and safety) ensure **safe**, **healthy**, and **fair** working conditions for all employees. The system emphasizes **prevention**, **employee participation**, and **continuous improvement** of workplace environments while protecting workers from **discrimination**, **harassment**, and **unsafe conditions**.

## Key Information

- **Who is protected**: All employees in Denmark regardless of employment type
- **When rights apply**: From first day of employment
- **Prerequisites**: Employment relationship under Danish law
- **Enforcement**: Labor inspectorate, unions, and legal system

## Fundamental Workplace Rights

### Right to Safe Working Environment

**Basic safety rights**:
- **Safe and healthy** workplace conditions
- **Proper safety** equipment and training
- **Risk assessment** and hazard prevention
- **Emergency procedures** and first aid access

### Right to Fair Treatment

**Equal treatment rights**:
- **Non-discrimination** based on protected characteristics
- **Equal pay** for equal work
- **Fair hiring** and promotion practices
- **Respectful workplace** culture

### Right to Information and Participation

**Employee involvement**:
- **Information about** workplace policies and procedures
- **Participation in** safety committees and workplace decisions
- **Consultation on** changes affecting working conditions
- **Access to** employment records and information

## Health and Safety Regulations

### Employer Obligations

**Legal requirements for employers**:
- **Provide safe** working environment
- **Conduct risk** assessments regularly
- **Provide safety** training and equipment
- **Report accidents** and near-misses
- **Maintain safety** records and documentation

### Employee Responsibilities

**Worker obligations**:
- **Follow safety** procedures and guidelines
- **Use provided** safety equipment properly
- **Report hazards** and unsafe conditions
- **Participate in** safety training programs
- **Cooperate with** safety representatives

### Safety Representatives

**Worker safety representation**:
- **Elected by** employees in workplaces with 10+ workers
- **Inspect workplace** conditions regularly
- **Investigate accidents** and safety concerns
- **Participate in** safety committee meetings
- **Stop dangerous** work when necessary

### Working Environment Committees

**Joint safety committees**:
- **Required in** workplaces with 35+ employees
- **Equal representation** of employers and employees
- **Regular meetings** to discuss safety issues
- **Authority to** make safety recommendations
- **Oversight of** safety policies and procedures

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

### Protected Characteristics

**Discrimination prohibited based on**:
- **Gender** and gender identity
- **Age** (both young and old)
- **Race, ethnicity,** and national origin
- **Religion** and belief systems
- **Sexual orientation**
- **Disability** and health conditions
- **Political opinions**
- **Pregnancy** and family status

### Types of Discrimination

**Prohibited discriminatory practices**:
- **Direct discrimination** - treating someone less favorably
- **Indirect discrimination** - policies that disproportionately affect protected groups
- **Harassment** - unwanted conduct related to protected characteristics
- **Victimization** - retaliation for making discrimination complaints

### Equal Pay Requirements

**Gender pay equity**:
- **Equal pay** for work of equal value
- **Transparent salary** structures and criteria
- **Regular pay** equity audits (companies with 35+ employees)
- **Justification required** for pay differences

## Harassment and Bullying Prevention

### Workplace Harassment

**Prohibited behaviors**:
- **Sexual harassment** and unwanted sexual attention
- **Psychological harassment** and bullying
- **Verbal abuse** and threatening behavior
- **Social exclusion** and isolation
- **Abuse of power** by supervisors

### Employer Prevention Duties

**Required prevention measures**:
- **Clear policies** against harassment and bullying
- **Training programs** for managers and employees
- **Complaint procedures** and investigation processes
- **Prompt action** to address reported incidents
- **Protection against** retaliation for reporting

### Reporting and Resolution

**How to address harassment**:
1. **Document incidents** with dates, witnesses, and details
2. **Report to** supervisor, HR, or safety representative
3. **File formal** complaint if informal resolution fails
4. **Seek union** assistance and legal advice
5. **Contact external** authorities if necessary

## Pregnancy and Family Rights

### Pregnancy Protection

**Rights during pregnancy**:
- **No discrimination** based on pregnancy
- **Reasonable accommodations** for pregnancy-related needs
- **Time off** for prenatal appointments
- **Safe working** conditions and risk assessments
- **Job protection** during pregnancy and maternity leave

### Parental Leave Rights

**Family leave protections**:
- **Maternity leave** (14 weeks for birth mothers)
- **Paternity leave** (2 weeks for partners)
- **Shared parental** leave (32 weeks to divide)
- **Job guarantee** upon return from leave
- **Flexible return** arrangements when possible

### Breastfeeding Support

**Nursing mother accommodations**:
- **Breastfeeding breaks** during work hours
- **Private space** for pumping/nursing
- **Flexible scheduling** for nursing needs
- **No penalty** for breastfeeding-related absences

## Workplace Surveillance and Privacy

### Employee Privacy Rights

**Privacy protections**:
- **Personal data** protection under GDPR
- **Limited surveillance** and monitoring
- **Private communications** respect
- **Medical information** confidentiality
- **Personal belongings** security

### Acceptable Monitoring

**Permitted employer monitoring**:
- **Work performance** and productivity tracking
- **Computer and internet** usage (with notice)
- **Security cameras** in common areas (not private spaces)
- **Time and attendance** tracking systems

### Data Protection

**Employee data rights**:
- **Access to** personal data held by employer
- **Correction of** inaccurate information
- **Deletion of** unnecessary personal data
- **Consent for** data processing beyond employment needs

## Whistleblower Protection

### Protected Disclosures

**Reportable concerns**:
- **Illegal activities** and law violations
- **Health and safety** hazards
- **Environmental violations**
- **Financial misconduct** and fraud
- **Discrimination** and harassment

### Legal Protections

**Whistleblower rights**:
- **Protection against** retaliation and dismissal
- **Confidentiality** of identity when possible
- **Legal immunity** for good faith reporting
- **Compensation** for retaliation damages

### Reporting Channels

**How to report concerns**:
- **Internal channels** (management, compliance officers)
- **External authorities** (labor inspectorate, police)
- **Anonymous hotlines** and reporting systems
- **Union representatives** and legal advisors

## Workplace Inspections and Enforcement

### Labor Inspectorate

**Government oversight**:
- **Workplace inspections** without prior notice
- **Investigation of** complaints and accidents
- **Enforcement of** safety and employment laws
- **Guidance and** education for employers and employees

### Inspection Process

**What happens during inspections**:
- **Review of** safety procedures and documentation
- **Interviews with** employees and management
- **Physical inspection** of workplace conditions
- **Recommendations** and improvement orders
- **Follow-up visits** to ensure compliance

### Penalties and Enforcement

**Consequences for violations**:
- **Improvement notices** with deadlines
- **Fines and** financial penalties
- **Work stoppages** for immediate dangers
- **Criminal prosecution** for serious violations

## Dispute Resolution

### Internal Procedures

**Workplace conflict resolution**:
- **Direct discussion** with supervisor or colleague
- **HR mediation** and conflict resolution
- **Grievance procedures** outlined in employment contracts
- **Union representation** in formal procedures

### External Support

**Outside assistance**:
- **Labor court** system for employment disputes
- **Equality and** non-discrimination board
- **Ombudsman services** for government employees
- **Legal aid** and professional representation

### Documentation and Evidence

**Protecting your rights**:
- **Keep detailed** records of incidents and communications
- **Collect witness** statements and supporting evidence
- **Follow formal** procedures and deadlines
- **Seek professional** advice for complex cases

## Special Protections for Vulnerable Workers

### Young Workers

**Additional protections for workers under 18**:
- **Restricted working** hours and night work
- **Prohibited dangerous** work and machinery
- **Enhanced safety** training and supervision
- **Educational priority** over work obligations

### Temporary and Agency Workers

**Rights for non-permanent workers**:
- **Same safety** protections as permanent employees
- **Equal treatment** in working conditions
- **Clear information** about job duties and risks
- **Access to** training and safety equipment

### Migrant Workers

**Protections for foreign workers**:
- **Same workplace** rights as Danish workers
- **Language support** for safety training
- **Protection against** exploitation and abuse
- **Access to** union representation and legal aid

### Green Workplace Initiatives
