# Working Hours Regulations and Overtime

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/arbejde-dagpenge-og-sygedagpenge/arbejdstid  
> **Applies to**: All Employees and Employers

## Overview

Danish **arbejdstidsregler** (working hours regulations) establish **maximum working hours**, **rest periods**, and **overtime compensation** to protect worker health and ensure **work-life balance**. The regulations apply to all employees while allowing **flexibility** through collective agreements and individual arrangements.

## Key Information

- **Who is covered**: All employees in Denmark (some exceptions for management)
- **When applicable**: All working time including overtime and on-call duties
- **Prerequisites**: Employment relationship under Danish law
- **Enforcement**: Labor inspectorate and collective agreement oversight

## Standard Working Hours

### Normal Working Week

**Standard work schedule**:
- **37 hours per week** typical full-time employment
- **Monday to Friday** traditional schedule
- **7.4 hours per day** average for 5-day week
- **Flexible arrangements** possible within limits

### Daily Working Time Limits

**Maximum daily hours**:
- **8 hours per day** standard limit
- **10 hours per day** maximum including overtime
- **13 hours per day** exceptional circumstances (with authority approval)
- **Average limits** calculated over reference periods

### Weekly Working Time Limits

**Maximum weekly hours**:
- **48 hours per week** including overtime (EU directive)
- **Average over 4 months** reference period
- **Collective agreements** may set lower limits
- **Individual opt-out** possible in limited circumstances

## Rest Periods and Breaks

### Daily Rest Requirements

**Mandatory rest between work days**:
- **11 hours continuous** rest between shifts
- **Exceptions possible** for certain sectors with compensatory rest
- **Shift work** special arrangements
- **On-call time** considerations

### Weekly Rest Requirements

**Weekly rest periods**:
- **24 hours continuous** rest per week
- **Usually Sunday** but flexible scheduling allowed
- **Can be averaged** over 14-day period
- **Religious and** cultural considerations

### Meal Breaks and Pauses

**Work break requirements**:
- **30 minutes break** for work periods over 6 hours
- **Unpaid break** unless specified in collective agreement
- **Flexible timing** by mutual agreement
- **Additional breaks** for demanding work

## Overtime Regulations

### Overtime Definition

**What counts as overtime**:
- **Work beyond** normal contracted hours
- **Work on** designated rest days
- **Emergency work** outside normal schedule
- **Training and** travel time (in some cases)

### Overtime Limits

**Maximum overtime allowed**:
- **250 hours per year** standard limit
- **Additional hours** with employee consent and authority approval
- **Collective agreements** may set different limits
- **Health and safety** considerations paramount

### Overtime Compensation

**Payment for extra hours**:
- **150% of normal** hourly rate (time and a half)
- **200% for work** on Sundays and holidays
- **Compensatory time off** as alternative (1.5 hours off per overtime hour)
- **Collective agreements** may specify higher rates

### Voluntary vs. Mandatory Overtime

**Employee rights**:
- **Voluntary basis** for most overtime
- **Reasonable notice** required for overtime requests
- **Emergency situations** may require mandatory overtime
- **Right to refuse** excessive or unreasonable overtime

## Special Working Time Arrangements

### Shift Work

**Rotating schedule work**:
- **Special rest period** requirements
- **Health assessments** for night shift workers
- **Shift premiums** and compensation
- **Rotation patterns** and frequency limits

### Night Work

**Work between 23:00-06:00**:
- **Maximum 8 hours** per night shift
- **Health surveillance** requirements
- **Night work premiums** (typically 15-25% extra)
- **Right to transfer** to day work when possible

### Weekend and Holiday Work

**Non-standard day work**:
- **Double pay rates** for Sunday work
- **Holiday premiums** for public holiday work
- **Compensatory time off** alternatives
- **Voluntary basis** except essential services

### On-Call and Standby

**Availability requirements**:
- **Standby time** compensation (typically 25% of hourly rate)
- **Call-out payments** for actual work performed
- **Response time** requirements
- **Rest period** protection after call-outs
