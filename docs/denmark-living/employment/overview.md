## Key Information

### Standard Working Conditions
- **Working week**: Typically 37 hours
- **Vacation**: Minimum 5 weeks (25 days) per year
- **Overtime**: Regulated by collective agreements
- **Notice period**: Varies by tenure (1-6 months)

### Union Membership
- **Coverage**: ~67% of Danish workers
- **Benefits**: Legal support, unemployment insurance, salary negotiations
- **A-kasse**: Unemployment insurance fund (often linked to union)
- **Cost**: Typically 400-600 DKK/month

### Salary Payment
- **Frequency**: Monthly (end of month or beginning of next)
- **Method**: Bank transfer to NemKonto
- **Pension**: Automatic contributions (typically 12-17% total)
- **Tax**: Withheld at source based on tax card

## Common Questions

### Do I need to join a union?

Not required, but highly recommended. Benefits include:
- Legal advice and support
- Unemployment insurance (A-kasse)
- Salary negotiation support
- Workplace representation

### What is an A-kasse?

Unemployment insurance fund that provides dagpenge (unemployment benefits) if you lose your job. Requires:
- Monthly membership fees
- Typically 1 year membership before claiming
- Active job search when unemployed

### How much vacation do I get?

Minimum 5 weeks (25 days) per year by law. Some collective agreements provide more. Vacation is earned during the year and typically taken the following year.

### What if I'm fired or want to quit?

Notice periods apply to both employer and employee:
- **Probation period**: Often 3 months with shorter notice
- **After probation**: 1-6 months depending on tenure
- **Collective agreements**: May specify different terms
