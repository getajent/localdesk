# Danish Language Courses

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://uim.dk/arbejdsomrader/Integration/danskuddannelse  
> **Applies to**: All International Residents

## Overview

**Danskuddannelse** (Danish education) provides structured language learning opportunities for international residents in Denmark. The program offers **free Danish language courses** to help newcomers integrate into Danish society, improve employment prospects, and participate fully in community life.

## Key Information

- **Who is eligible**: All international residents with residence permits
- **When to start**: As soon as possible after receiving CPR number
- **Prerequisites**: CPR number, residence permit, registration with municipality
- **Processing time**: 1-2 weeks for enrollment, courses start regularly throughout year

## Types of Danish Courses

### Danskuddannelse (Danish Education)

**Official government program** providing:
- **Free tuition** for eligible residents
- **Structured curriculum** from beginner to advanced levels
- **Qualified teachers** with specialized training
- **Flexible scheduling** including evening and weekend options
- **Childcare support** during class hours (at some locations)

### Course Levels

**Danish Education 1 (DU1)**
- For participants with **limited formal education**
- Focus on basic literacy and numeracy
- Slower pace with extra support
- Practical Danish for daily life

**Danish Education 2 (DU2)**
- For participants with **short formal education** (up to 7-8 years)
- Basic Danish language skills
- Practical communication focus
- Integration into Danish society

**Danish Education 3 (DU3)**
- For participants with **longer formal education** (9+ years)
- Academic approach to language learning
- Faster progression through levels
- Preparation for higher education or professional work

### Proficiency Levels

Each education track includes multiple levels:
- **Beginner levels**: A1, A2 (basic communication)
- **Intermediate levels**: B1, B2 (independent use)
- **Advanced levels**: C1, C2 (proficient use)

## Eligibility and Enrollment

### Who Can Enroll

**Eligible participants**:
- **Refugees** and family reunification cases
- **EU citizens** residing in Denmark
- **Non-EU citizens** with residence permits
- **Spouses** of Danish citizens
- **Students** (limited eligibility)

### Enrollment Process

1. **Contact Municipality**
   - Visit your local **municipality integration office**
   - Bring CPR card and residence permit
   - Complete enrollment application
   - Take placement test to determine appropriate level

2. **Assessment and Placement**
   - **Language assessment** to determine current level
   - **Educational background** evaluation
   - **Learning goals** discussion
   - **Course track assignment** (DU1, DU2, or DU3)

3. **Course Assignment**
   - **School selection** based on location and availability
   - **Schedule coordination** with work or other commitments
   - **Start date** confirmation
   - **Materials** and textbook information

### Required Documents

- **CPR card** or certificate
- **Residence permit** or EU documentation
- **Educational certificates** (translated if necessary)
- **Employment information** (if applicable)
- **Previous Danish course** certificates (if any)

## Course Structure and Content

### Class Schedule

**Full-time courses**:
- **20-25 hours per week**
- Monday through Friday
- Morning or afternoon sessions
- Intensive progression

**Part-time courses**:
- **6-12 hours per week**
- Evening classes available
- Weekend options at some schools
- Flexible for working students

### Curriculum Content

**Language skills**:
- **Speaking and listening** comprehension
- **Reading and writing** skills
- **Grammar and vocabulary** development
- **Pronunciation** and intonation

**Cultural integration**:
- **Danish society** and culture
- **Work culture** and professional communication
- **Civic education** and democratic values
- **Practical life skills** in Denmark

### Teaching Methods

- **Communicative approach** emphasizing practical use
- **Task-based learning** with real-life scenarios
- **Digital tools** and online resources
- **Group work** and peer interaction
- **Individual support** for different learning needs

## Costs and Financial Support

### Free Tuition

- **No tuition fees** for eligible participants
- **Free textbooks** and learning materials
- **No registration fees** or administrative costs

### Additional Support

**Childcare assistance**:
- **Free childcare** during class hours (where available)
- **Transportation support** in some municipalities
- **Flexible scheduling** for parents

**Financial support**:
- **Integration allowance** may be available
- **Continued unemployment benefits** while attending (if eligible)
- **Student discounts** for public transportation

## Language Schools and Providers

### Municipal Language Schools

Most municipalities operate their own language centers:
- **Københavns Sprogcenter** (Copenhagen)
- **Aarhus Kommunes Sprogcenter** (Aarhus)
- **Odense Sprogskole** (Odense)
- **Aalborg Sprogcenter** (Aalborg)

### Private Providers

Authorized private schools also offer danskuddannelse:
- **AOF** (Workers' Educational Association)
- **FOF** (Association for Popular Education)
- **Studieskolen** (various locations)
- **Berlitz** (Copenhagen, Aarhus)

### Online and Hybrid Options

- **Digital learning platforms** supplementing classroom instruction
- **Virtual classrooms** for remote areas
- **Self-paced modules** for flexible learning
- **Mobile apps** for vocabulary and practice

## Certification and Testing

### Module Tests

- **Regular assessments** throughout each level
- **Oral and written** components
- **Pass/fail** grading system
- **Retake opportunities** if needed

### Final Examinations

**Prøve i Dansk (PD)**:
- **Official certification** of Danish proficiency
- **Required for citizenship** application
- **Recognized by employers** and educational institutions
- **Multiple test dates** throughout the year

### Certification Levels

- **PD1**: Basic level (A2 equivalent)
- **PD2**: Intermediate level (B1 equivalent)
- **PD3**: Advanced level (B2+ equivalent)

## Tips for Success

### Maximize Learning

- **Attend regularly** - consistency is key
- **Practice daily** outside of class
- **Engage with Danes** in everyday situations
- **Use Danish media** (TV, radio, newspapers)
- **Join conversation groups** and language exchanges

### Integration Opportunities

- **Volunteer work** to practice Danish
- **Sports clubs** and hobby groups
- **Parent groups** if you have children
- **Workplace Danish** practice with colleagues

## Common Questions

### How long does it take to learn Danish?

**Timeline varies** based on:
- Previous language learning experience
- Time dedicated to study and practice
- Educational background
- Opportunities for daily Danish use
- Individual learning pace

Typical progression: **1-3 years** to reach conversational level.

### Can I work while taking Danish courses?

**Yes**, many students combine work and language learning. Part-time courses are designed for working students.

### What if I miss classes?

**Regular attendance** is important for progression. Contact your school about:
- **Make-up sessions** for missed classes
- **Extra support** if falling behind
- **Flexible scheduling** options
