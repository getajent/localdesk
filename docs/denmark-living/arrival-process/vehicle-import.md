# Vehicle Import and Driving License Exchange

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/transport-og-trafik/koretoj-og-korekort  
> **Applies to**: All Residents with Vehicles

## Overview

**Køretøj import** (vehicle import) and **kørekort ombytning** (driving license exchange) are required procedures for residents bringing vehicles to Denmark or needing to exchange foreign driving licenses. You have **30 days** after establishing residency to register vehicles and may need to exchange your driving license depending on your country of origin.

## Key Information

- **Who needs this**: Residents importing vehicles or holding foreign driving licenses
- **When to do this**: Within 30 days of establishing Danish residency
- **Prerequisites**: CPR number, vehicle documentation, valid foreign license
- **Processing time**: 2-4 weeks for vehicle registration, 1-2 weeks for license exchange

## Vehicle Import and Registration

### 30-Day Deadline

**Critical deadline**: You must register your vehicle within **30 days** of establishing residency in Denmark. Failure to do so results in significant penalties.

### Required Documents for Vehicle Import

- **Vehicle registration certificate** from country of origin
- **Proof of ownership** (purchase receipt, loan documents)
- **Valid insurance** covering Denmark
- **Technical inspection certificate** (if required)
- **CPR number** and proof of Danish residency
- **Import declaration** (for non-EU vehicles)

### Registration Process

1. **Technical Inspection** (if required)
   - Book appointment at **Bilsyn Danmark** or authorized center
   - Bring vehicle and all documentation
   - Pay inspection fee (approximately 500-800 DKK)
   - Receive inspection certificate

2. **Tax Assessment**
   - **Registration tax** (registreringsafgift) calculated based on vehicle value
   - **EU vehicles**: Lower tax rates apply
   - **Non-EU vehicles**: Higher import duties and taxes
   - Use SKAT's online calculator for estimates

3. **Insurance Requirement**
   - Obtain **Danish motor insurance** before registration
   - Minimum third-party liability coverage required
   - Compare providers: Tryg, Alka, If, Codan, others
   - Provide insurance certificate during registration

4. **Submit Registration**
   - **Online**: Through borger.dk (requires MitID)
   - **In person**: At SKAT office or authorized dealer
   - Pay registration tax and fees
   - Receive Danish license plates and registration certificate

### Where to Register

- **Online**: borger.dk (for eligible vehicles)
- **SKAT offices**: For complex cases or assistance
- **Authorized dealers**: Many car dealers handle registration
- **Bilsyn centers**: Combined inspection and registration services

## Driving License Exchange

### EU/EEA License Exchange

**EU citizens** can exchange licenses from EU/EEA countries:

1. **Application Process**
   - Apply online through borger.dk
   - Submit original foreign license
   - Provide CPR number and proof of residency
   - Pay fee (approximately 250 DKK)

2. **Required Documents**
   - Original EU/EEA driving license
   - CPR certificate
   - Passport or ID card
   - Proof of Danish address
   - Recent passport photo

### Non-EU License Exchange

**Non-EU licenses** have different requirements:

1. **Eligible Countries**
   - Some countries have **reciprocal agreements** with Denmark
   - Check current list on borger.dk
   - May require additional testing or documentation

2. **Ineligible Countries**
   - Must take **Danish driving test**
   - Both theoretical and practical exams required
   - Consider taking driving lessons in Danish

### Temporary Driving Rights

- **EU licenses**: Valid for permanent use until exchanged
- **Non-EU licenses**: Valid for **90 days** after establishing residency
- **International Driving Permit**: May extend validity temporarily

## Costs and Fees

### Vehicle Registration Costs

- **Registration tax**: 25-180% of vehicle value (varies by type and emissions)
- **Registration fee**: Approximately 1,180 DKK
- **License plates**: Approximately 1,000 DKK
- **Technical inspection**: 500-800 DKK (if required)

### License Exchange Costs

- **EU license exchange**: Approximately 250 DKK
- **Non-EU license exchange**: 250-500 DKK (varies by country)
- **Driving test** (if required): 600-1,200 DKK
- **Driving lessons**: 400-600 DKK per lesson

## Special Considerations

### Electric and Hybrid Vehicles

- **Reduced registration tax** for environmentally friendly vehicles
- **Special incentives** may apply
- Check current environmental vehicle policies

### Motorcycles and Special Vehicles

- **Different requirements** for motorcycles, trailers, and commercial vehicles
- **Additional licenses** may be required for certain vehicle types
- Consult SKAT for specific vehicle category requirements

### Temporary Import

- **Short-term visitors**: May use foreign plates temporarily
- **Work assignments**: Special rules for temporary residents
- **Tourist vehicles**: Different regulations apply

## Common Questions

### What if I miss the 30-day deadline?

You will face **significant penalties** including fines and back-taxes. Contact SKAT immediately to minimize penalties and complete registration as soon as possible.

### Can I drive while waiting for license exchange?

**EU licenses**: Yes, you can continue driving with your EU license while the exchange is processed.
**Non-EU licenses**: Only for 90 days after establishing residency.

### Do I need a Danish driving test?

**EU/EEA citizens**: No, simple exchange process.
**Non-EU citizens**: Depends on your country of origin and reciprocal agreements.

### What about vehicle insurance?

You **must** have Danish motor insurance before registering your vehicle. Your foreign insurance typically does not provide adequate coverage under Danish law.

## Important Deadlines

- **Vehicle registration**: 30 days after establishing residency
- **Non-EU license validity**: 90 days after establishing residency
- **Insurance requirement**: Must be in place before driving in Denmark
