# GP Registration (Family Doctor Selection)

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/sundhed-og-sygdom/laegevalg  
> **Applies to**: All Residents

## Overview

**Lægevalg** (GP selection) is the process of choosing and registering with a family doctor in Denmark. All residents with a **CPR-nummer** (civil registration number) must select a general practitioner who will serve as their primary healthcare contact and coordinate referrals to specialists.

## Key Information

- **Who needs this**: All residents with CPR number and yellow health insurance card
- **When to do this**: After receiving CPR number and health insurance card
- **Prerequisites**: Valid CPR number, health insurance card (yellow card)
- **Processing time**: Immediate online registration, appointment availability varies

## How to Choose a GP

### Finding Available GPs

1. Visit the **Lægevalg** website at sundhed.dk
2. Enter your address or postal code
3. View list of GPs accepting new patients in your area
4. Check GP information including:
   - Languages spoken
   - Practice hours
   - Special services offered
   - Patient reviews and ratings

### Selection Criteria

Consider these factors when choosing:
- **Location**: Proximity to home or work
- **Languages**: GPs who speak your preferred language
- **Specializations**: Doctors with relevant expertise
- **Practice type**: Solo practice vs. group practice
- **Appointment availability**: How quickly you can get appointments

## Registration Process

### Required Information

- CPR number
- Health insurance card number
- Contact information (address, phone, email)
- Preferred GP choice (doctor name and practice address)

### Registration Steps

1. **Online Registration** (Recommended)
   - Go to sundhed.dk
   - Log in with **MitID**
   - Navigate to "Lægevalg" (GP Selection)
   - Search for available GPs in your area
   - Select your preferred GP
   - Confirm registration

2. **Phone Registration**
   - Call your chosen GP practice directly
   - Provide CPR number and personal information
   - Confirm acceptance as new patient
   - Schedule initial appointment if needed

3. **In-Person Registration**
   - Visit the GP practice during office hours
   - Bring CPR card and health insurance card
   - Complete registration forms
   - Schedule initial appointment

### Where to Register

- **Online**: sundhed.dk (requires MitID)
- **Phone**: Contact GP practice directly
- **In Person**: Visit chosen GP practice

## After Registration

### Initial Appointment

- Schedule a **sundhedstjek** (health check) within first few months
- Discuss medical history and current health status
- Establish care plan and preferences
- Get information about practice procedures

### Changing GPs

You can change your GP:
- **Once per month** without restriction
- **Immediately** if moving to new area
- **Anytime** if current GP stops accepting patients

To change:
1. Select new GP through sundhed.dk
2. New registration automatically cancels previous GP
3. Medical records transfer automatically

## Common Questions

### Can I choose any GP in Denmark?

No, you must choose a GP in your **region** (healthcare region). You can see which GPs are available in your area through the lægevalg system.

### What if no GPs are accepting new patients?

Contact your **regional health authority** for assistance. They can help find available GPs or place you on waiting lists.

### Do I need to speak Danish?

Many GPs speak English, especially in larger cities. You can filter by language on sundhed.dk when selecting a GP.

### What if I need urgent care before registering?

Visit the nearest **akutmodtagelse** (emergency department) or call **1813** for urgent but non-emergency medical advice.

## Important Information

### GP Services Include

- General health consultations
- Preventive care and health screenings
- Referrals to specialists
- Prescription medications
- Sick leave certificates
- Vaccination services

### Costs

- GP visits are **free** with health insurance card
- Some services may require small co-payments
- Private consultations (without referral) may have fees
