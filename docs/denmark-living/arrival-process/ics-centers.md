# International Citizen Service (ICS) Centers

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://newtodenmark.dk/guides/ics-international-citizen-service  
> **Applies to**: All International Newcomers

## Overview

**International Citizen Service (ICS)** centers provide comprehensive support and guidance for international citizens moving to Denmark. These centers offer **free assistance** with essential registration processes, practical information, and help navigating Danish bureaucracy during your first months in the country.

## Key Information

- **Who can use this**: All international citizens moving to Denmark
- **When to visit**: As soon as possible after arrival, ideally within first weeks
- **Prerequisites**: None - services are free and open to all newcomers
- **Processing time**: Same-day assistance for most services

## Services Provided

### Registration Assistance

ICS centers help with:
- **CPR number application** guidance and support
- **MitID registration** assistance
- **Health insurance card** application help
- **Tax card** application support
- **Digital Post** activation assistance
- **Bank account opening** guidance

### Information and Guidance

- **Danish bureaucracy** explanation and navigation
- **Essential services** overview and access information
- **Rights and obligations** as a Danish resident
- **Integration programs** and language course information
- **Employment** guidance and job search support
- **Housing** information and rental market guidance

### Practical Support

- **Translation services** for important documents
- **Appointment booking** assistance for various agencies
- **Form completion** help for government applications
- **Document verification** and guidance
- **Referrals** to relevant authorities and services

## How to Use ICS Services

### Walk-in Services

- **No appointment needed** for general information
- **First-come, first-served** basis
- **Bring identification** (passport, visa, etc.)
- **Arrive early** during busy periods

### Appointment Services

- **Complex cases** may require appointments
- **Translation services** typically need advance booking
- **Specialized guidance** for specific situations
- **Book online** or call center directly

### What to Bring

**Essential documents**:
- **Passport** with valid visa/residence permit
- **Proof of address** in Denmark
- **Employment contract** or study enrollment (if applicable)
- **Marriage certificate** (if applicable)
- **Birth certificates** for children (if applicable)

## Common Questions

### Is ICS assistance really free?

Yes, all ICS services are **completely free**. The centers are funded by the Danish government to support international newcomers.

### Do I need to speak Danish?

No, ICS staff speak **English** and often other languages. Translation services are available for complex procedures.

### Can ICS help with private matters?

ICS focuses on **official procedures** and government services. For private matters like housing contracts or employment disputes, they can provide referrals to appropriate services.

### How long can I use ICS services?

There's **no time limit**, but services are primarily designed for newcomers in their first 1-2 years in Denmark.

## Tips for Using ICS

### Prepare for Your Visit

- **List your questions** in advance
- **Bring all relevant documents**
- **Allow sufficient time** - complex cases may take hours
- **Take notes** during your visit

### Follow-up Actions

- **Keep contact information** for future questions
- **Follow through** on recommended next steps
- **Return for updates** on ongoing applications
- **Refer friends** and family to ICS services
