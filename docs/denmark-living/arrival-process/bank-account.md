## Overview

Opening a Danish bank account is essential for living and working in Denmark. A bank account enables you to receive salary payments, pay rent and bills, use MobilePay for everyday transactions, and access Danish financial services. Additionally, you must register a **NemKonto** (Easy Account) to receive payments from Danish public authorities including tax refunds, social benefits, and government reimbursements.

## Key Information

- **Who needs this**: Anyone living or working in Denmark who needs to receive salary, pay bills, or receive government payments
- **When to do this**: After receiving your CPR number (required by most banks)
- **Prerequisites**: 
  - CPR number (civil registration number)
  - Valid passport or photo ID
  - Proof of address in Denmark (rental contract, utility bill, or health insurance card)
  - Employment contract or student documentation (often required)
- **Processing time**: 1-3 weeks for account approval; immediate online access once approved
- **Cost**: Varies by bank; some offer free accounts, others charge monthly fees (typically DKK 0-100/month)

## Why You Need a Danish Bank Account

A Danish bank account is necessary for:

- **Receiving salary**: Employers pay salaries directly to Danish bank accounts
- **Paying rent and utilities**: Most landlords and utility companies require Danish bank transfers
- **Using MobilePay**: Denmark's ubiquitous mobile payment app requires a Danish bank account
- **Receiving government payments**: Tax refunds, benefits, and reimbursements go to your NemKonto
- **Daily transactions**: Many shops, services, and online platforms prefer Danish payment methods
- **Dankort access**: Denmark's national debit card system for purchases and ATM withdrawals
- **Avoiding fees**: Using foreign bank accounts for Danish transactions incurs high conversion and transfer fees

Denmark is largely cashless, with most transactions conducted via card or mobile payment. Having a Danish bank account is essential for participating in daily life.

## Prerequisites

Before opening a bank account, ensure you have:

### Essential Requirements
1. **CPR number** (civil registration number)
   - Most banks require a CPR number before opening an account
   - Some banks may open accounts with a pending CPR application, but this is rare
2. **Valid photo identification**:
   - Passport (Danish or foreign)
   - National ID card (EU/EEA)
   - Danish driver's license
   - Residence permit with photo
3. **Proof of address in Denmark**:
   - Rental contract or lease agreement
   - Recent utility bill (electricity, water, internet)
   - Health insurance card (yellow card) showing your address
   - Letter from landlord confirming your residence
4. **Employment or study documentation**:
   - Employment contract or letter from employer
   - Student enrollment letter from educational institution
   - Letter of introduction from HR department (recommended)

### Additional Documents (May Be Required)
- **EU residence document** (for EU/EEA/Swiss citizens)
- **Residence and work permit** (for non-EU citizens)
- **Tax card** (skattekort) if already obtained
- **Proof of income** or source of funds (for anti-money laundering compliance)

## How to Open a Bank Account

### Steps to Open an Account

1. **Choose a bank**
   - Research banks and compare fees, services, and branch locations
   - Consider proximity to your home or workplace
   - Check if the bank has experience with international customers

2. **Gather required documents**
   - Collect all necessary identification and proof documents
   - Make copies if needed (some banks require originals)
   - Ensure your CPR number is ready

3. **Book an appointment**
   - Most banks require appointments for account opening
   - Book online through the bank's website or call the branch
   - Walk-ins are uncommon, especially during peak periods
   - Appointment wait times can be 1-2 weeks

4. **Attend your appointment**
   - Arrive on time with all required documents
   - Bank staff will verify your identity and documents
   - Complete Know Your Customer (KYC) and anti-money laundering checks
   - Provide information about your employment, income, and purpose for the account

5. **Sign account agreement**
   - Review account terms, fees, and conditions
   - Sign the account opening agreement (often digitally with MitID)
   - Choose account type and services (checking, savings, debit card, etc.)

6. **Wait for approval**
   - Banks typically process applications within 1-3 weeks
   - You may receive provisional access while final approval is pending
   - Some banks offer faster processing for students or employees of partner companies

7. **Receive account details and cards**
   - Once approved, you'll receive your IBAN and account number
   - Online banking credentials will be sent (usually via Digital Post or email)
   - Debit card (Dankort or Visa/Dankort) will be mailed to your address (5-10 business days)
   - PIN code for card will be sent separately for security

### Where to Apply

**In-Person at Bank Branches**:
- Visit your chosen bank's branch location
- Book an appointment in advance
- Bring all required documents

**Online Application** (Limited Availability):
- Some digital banks (Lunar, Revolut) offer online-only account opening
- Traditional banks may allow online applications but typically require in-person verification
- Requires MitID for digital signature (which requires CPR number)

**Through Employer or University**:
- Some employers and universities have partnerships with banks
- May offer expedited account opening or special services
- Ask your HR department or international office for assistance

**Bank Hours**:
- Most banks open Monday-Friday, 10:00-16:00
- Extended hours on Thursdays until 17:00 or 17:30
- Online banking available 24/7

## What Happens Next

### Immediate Access
- **Online banking**: Access your account via web or mobile app
- **Account number and IBAN**: Use for receiving payments and transfers
- **Temporary access**: Some banks provide provisional access while cards are being delivered

### Within 1-2 Weeks
- **Debit card arrives**: Dankort or Visa/Dankort card mailed to your address
- **PIN code arrives**: Sent separately from card for security
- **Full account activation**: Complete access to all banking services

### Next Steps After Opening Account
1. **Register NemKonto**: Designate your account as your NemKonto for government payments
2. **Set up MobilePay**: Download and link MobilePay to your Danish bank account
3. **Provide account details to employer**: Give IBAN to HR for salary payments
4. **Set up automatic payments**: Arrange rent, utilities, and bill payments
5. **Order additional services**: Request credit card, overdraft, or savings account if needed

## Understanding Danish Banking Services

### Dankort
**Dankort** is Denmark's national debit card system, widely accepted throughout the country.

- **What it is**: A debit card linked directly to your Danish bank account
- **Usage**: Accepted at virtually all Danish shops, restaurants, and services
- **ATM access**: Withdraw cash at Danish ATMs (usually free at your own bank's ATMs)
- **Fees**: Typically included with your bank account; no per-transaction fees for cardholders
- **Visa/Dankort**: Combined card that works as Dankort in Denmark and Visa internationally

**Recommendation**: Request a Visa/Dankort for maximum flexibility in Denmark and abroad.

### MobilePay
**MobilePay** is Denmark's most popular mobile payment app, used by over 4 million Danes.

- **What it is**: Mobile payment app for person-to-person transfers and merchant payments
- **Usage**: Pay friends, split bills, pay in shops, restaurants, and online
- **Requirements**: Danish bank account and Danish phone number
- **Fees**: Free for personal use; small fees for business accounts (DKK 0.30-0.80 per transaction)
- **Setup**: Download app, link to your bank account, verify with MitID

**Why you need it**: MobilePay is ubiquitous in Denmark for splitting bills, paying friends, and making everyday purchases. Many small businesses and services only accept MobilePay or card payments.

### Online Banking
All Danish banks offer comprehensive online banking:

- **Access**: Web browser or mobile app
- **Login**: Requires MitID for secure authentication
- **Services**: View balances, transfer money, pay bills, manage cards, view statements
- **Language**: Major banks offer English-language interfaces
- **Availability**: 24/7 access

### Account Types
- **Checking account** (Lønkonto): Standard account for salary, bills, and daily transactions
- **Savings account** (Opsparingskonto): Higher interest rates, limited transactions
- **Youth account**: Special accounts for students under 25 (often with lower fees)
- **Joint account**: Shared account for couples or families

### Fees and Charges
Bank fees vary significantly:

- **Monthly account fee**: DKK 0-100/month (some banks offer free accounts)
- **Debit card fee**: Usually included or DKK 0-50/year
- **ATM withdrawals**: Free at own bank's ATMs; fees at other banks (DKK 20-40)
- **International transfers**: Varies by bank and transfer method
- **Overdraft fees**: High interest rates if you overdraw (avoid if possible)

**Tip**: Compare fees across banks before choosing. Students and young people often qualify for free or reduced-fee accounts.

## NemKonto (Easy Account)

### What is NemKonto?

**NemKonto** (Easy Account) is a designated bank account registered with the Danish government for receiving public payments. All residents and companies in Denmark are required to have a NemKonto.

- **Purpose**: Receive payments from Danish public authorities
- **Payments received**: Tax refunds, social benefits (boligstøtte, dagpenge, børnepenge), government reimbursements, salary from public employers
- **Requirement**: Mandatory for all residents registered in the CPR system
- **Setup**: Any Danish bank account can be designated as your NemKonto

### Why You Need NemKonto

Without a registered NemKonto:
- You cannot receive tax refunds from SKAT
- You cannot receive social benefits (housing benefits, unemployment benefits, child benefits)
- You cannot receive government reimbursements or payments
- Some employers (especially public sector) cannot pay your salary

### How to Register NemKonto

#### Method 1: Through Your Bank (Easiest)

1. **Open a Danish bank account** (as described above)
2. **Ask your bank to register it as your NemKonto**
   - Most banks automatically register your primary account as NemKonto
   - Or request it during account opening
3. **Confirmation**
   - Bank will register the account with the NemKonto system
   - You'll receive confirmation via Digital Post or letter

#### Method 2: Online via NemKonto Self-Service

1. **Log in to NemKonto self-service**
   - Visit [nemkonto.dk](https://www.nemkonto.dk/)
   - Log in with MitID (requires CPR number)
2. **Select your bank account**
   - Choose which of your Danish bank accounts to designate as NemKonto
   - Enter account number and registration number
3. **Confirm registration**
   - Submit your selection
   - Receive confirmation immediately

#### Method 3: Register Foreign Bank Account (If No Danish Account)

If you don't have a Danish bank account yet, you can temporarily register a foreign bank account as your NemKonto:

1. **Download registration form**
   - Visit [nemkonto.dk](https://www.nemkonto.dk/) and download the English registration form
2. **Complete the form**
   - Enter personal information (name, CPR number, address)
   - Enter bank information (IBAN, SWIFT/BIC code, account number)
   - Note: Not all countries use IBAN/SWIFT; some use account number and bank code
3. **Sign the form**
   - Sign in front of two witnesses OR a notary/attorney
   - Witnesses or notary must complete their sections of the form
   - Dates must match between signee and witnesses
4. **Provide identification**
   - Include a copy of your passport or official ID
   - Witnesses do not need to provide ID
5. **Submit the form**
   - Submit via online contact form at nemkonto.dk
   - Or mail to: The Agency for Digital Government, Att.: NemKonto Support, Slotsgade 1, 4800 Nykøbing F, Denmark
6. **Receive activation letter**
   - Activation letter will be sent to your registered CPR address
   - Follow instructions to activate your NemKonto

**Processing time**: Several weeks for foreign account registration

**Recommendation**: Open a Danish bank account as soon as possible and register it as your NemKonto. Foreign accounts have limitations and higher fees for receiving Danish payments.

### Managing Your NemKonto

- **View your NemKonto**: Log in to [nemkonto.dk](https://www.nemkonto.dk/) with MitID
- **Change your NemKonto**: Select a different bank account if you switch banks
- **Check payment history**: See payments received from public authorities
- **Update information**: Ensure your address is correct for receiving activation letters

## Common Questions

### Can I open a bank account without a CPR number?

Most Danish banks require a CPR number to open an account. However, some banks may:
- Open an account with a pending CPR application (rare and bank-dependent)
- Offer limited services until CPR number is obtained
- Require additional documentation or higher fees

**Recommendation**: Obtain your CPR number first, then open a bank account. This ensures a smooth process and full access to services.

### How long does it take to open a bank account?

- **Appointment booking**: 1-2 weeks wait time
- **Application processing**: 1-3 weeks after appointment
- **Card delivery**: 5-10 business days after approval
- **Total time**: Typically 2-5 weeks from initial contact to full account access

Digital banks like Lunar may offer faster processing (sometimes same-day approval).

### What if I don't have proof of address yet?

If you're staying temporarily or don't have a rental contract:
- **Hotel or temporary accommodation**: Provide booking confirmation or letter from host
- **Employer letter**: Some banks accept a letter from your employer confirming your employment and temporary address
- **Health insurance card**: Once you have your CPR number, your yellow card shows your registered address

Contact the bank in advance to discuss acceptable alternatives.

### Can I use my foreign bank account instead?

While possible, using a foreign bank account in Denmark has significant disadvantages:
- **High fees**: International transfer fees and currency conversion costs
- **Delays**: Payments take longer to process
- **Limited acceptance**: Many Danish services require Danish bank accounts
- **No MobilePay**: Cannot use MobilePay without a Danish account
- **Employer issues**: Some employers cannot pay salaries to foreign accounts

**Recommendation**: Open a Danish bank account as soon as possible for full integration.

### What is the difference between Dankort and Visa?

- **Dankort**: Danish national debit card, accepted only in Denmark, no per-transaction fees for cardholders
- **Visa**: International card network, accepted worldwide, may have foreign transaction fees
- **Visa/Dankort**: Combined card that functions as Dankort in Denmark and Visa abroad (best option)

Most banks issue Visa/Dankort cards by default.

### Do I need to register NemKonto separately?

If your bank automatically registers your account as NemKonto (most do), you don't need to do anything. However, you should:
- **Verify registration**: Log in to [nemkonto.dk](https://www.nemkonto.dk/) to confirm your NemKonto is registered
- **Update if needed**: If you switch banks, update your NemKonto to the new account

### Can I have multiple bank accounts?

Yes, you can have accounts at multiple banks. However:
- **Only one NemKonto**: You can only designate one account as your NemKonto
- **Primary account**: Choose your main account for salary and bills as your NemKonto
- **Secondary accounts**: Use for savings, investments, or specific purposes

### What if I'm a student?

Students often qualify for:
- **Free or reduced-fee accounts**: Many banks offer special student accounts
- **Faster processing**: Some banks prioritize student applications
- **University partnerships**: Check if your university has a partner bank

Bring your student enrollment letter and proof of study when opening an account.

### How do I close my bank account when leaving Denmark?

1. **Notify your bank**: Inform them of your departure date
2. **Transfer funds**: Move money to your home country account
3. **Cancel services**: Stop automatic payments, return cards
4. **Close account**: Complete closure forms (may require in-person visit or MitID signature)
5. **Update NemKonto**: If you're leaving permanently, you may need to deregister your NemKonto

## Important Deadlines

- **No specific deadline**: However, open a bank account as soon as you have a CPR number
- **Before first salary**: Ensure your account is open and provide IBAN to your employer before your first payday
- **NemKonto registration**: Register within a few weeks of opening your account to avoid delays in receiving government payments

## Troubleshooting

### Bank Rejects My Application

**Problem**: Bank refuses to open an account  
**Solution**:
- **Ask for specific reasons**: Banks must explain why they reject applications
- **Provide additional documentation**: Offer more proof of identity, address, or income
- **Try another bank**: Different banks have different policies; apply elsewhere
- **Seek assistance**: Contact International Citizen Service or your employer's HR department for help

### Cannot Get Appointment

**Problem**: Bank appointments are fully booked for weeks  
**Solution**:
- **Try multiple branches**: Different locations may have earlier availability
- **Check online**: Some banks offer online account opening
- **Consider digital banks**: Lunar and other digital banks have faster processes
- **Ask employer**: Your company may have a partnership with a bank for expedited service

### Debit Card Not Arriving

**Problem**: Card hasn't arrived after 2+ weeks  
**Solution**:
- **Check address**: Verify the bank has your correct address
- **Contact bank**: Call or visit to check card status
- **Request replacement**: Bank can issue a new card if the original was lost in mail
- **Use online banking**: Access your account online while waiting for card

### MobilePay Setup Fails

**Problem**: Cannot link MobilePay to bank account  
**Solution**:
- **Verify account is active**: Ensure your bank account is fully activated
- **Check phone number**: MobilePay requires a Danish phone number
- **Update app**: Ensure you have the latest version of MobilePay
- **Contact bank**: Some banks require activation of MobilePay integration
- **Try again later**: Technical issues may resolve after a few hours

### NemKonto Not Registered

**Problem**: NemKonto doesn't appear in the system  
**Solution**:
- **Wait for processing**: Registration can take a few days
- **Check with bank**: Confirm they submitted the registration
- **Register manually**: Use [nemkonto.dk](https://www.nemkonto.dk/) to register yourself
- **Verify CPR number**: Ensure your CPR number is correct in the system

### What if my account opening is rejected?

If your application is rejected, common reasons include:
- **Insufficient documentation**: Provide additional proof of identity, address, or income
- **CPR number issues**: Ensure your CPR number is valid and registered
- **Anti-money laundering concerns**: Be prepared to explain source of funds or employment
- **Bank policy**: Some banks have stricter requirements for international customers

Try applying at a different bank or seek assistance from International Citizen Service.
