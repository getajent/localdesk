## Overview

Denmark has a comprehensive tax system with income tax, labor market contributions, and municipal taxes. Understanding your tax obligations, available deductions, and the annual tax return process is essential for all residents with income in Denmark.

## Key Information

### Tax Rates (2025)
- **AM-bidrag**: 8% of gross income
- **Bottom tax**: ~12% (after AM-bidrag)
- **Municipal tax**: ~25% average (varies by municipality)
- **Top tax**: 15% on income above ~568,900 DKK
- **Effective rate**: Typically 35-52% depending on income

### Important Dates
- **March**: Preliminary tax return (forskudsopgørelse) available
- **May**: Annual tax return (årsopgørelse) available
- **June-July**: Deadline to correct tax return
- **Throughout year**: Tax paid via withholding from salary

### Tax Cards
- **Hovedkort** (main card): For primary employment
- **Bikort** (secondary card): For additional income (higher tax rate)
- **Frikort** (free card): For small amounts of income (tax-free up to limit)

## Common Questions

### How much tax will I pay?

Depends on your income and municipality. Example for 400,000 DKK annual income:
- AM-bidrag: 32,000 DKK (8%)
- Income tax: ~130,000 DKK (35% effective)
- Total: ~162,000 DKK (40.5% total effective rate)

Higher incomes pay more due to top tax bracket.

### What is AM-bidrag?

Labor market contribution (arbejdsmarkedsbidrag) - 8% tax on gross income that funds unemployment benefits and labor market programs. Deducted before income tax calculation.

### Do I need to file a tax return?

Not actively - SKAT prepares your annual tax return (årsopgørelse) automatically based on information from employers, banks, etc. You must:
- Review it when available (usually May)
- Correct any errors
- Pay additional tax or receive refund

### What deductions can I claim?

Common deductions:
- **Transport**: Commuting costs above 1,250 DKK/year
- **Pension contributions**: Contributions to approved pension schemes
- **Union fees**: A-kasse and union membership
- **Interest**: Mortgage interest (limited deduction)
- **Charitable donations**: To approved organizations

### What if I work in Denmark but live abroad (or vice versa)?

Special rules apply for cross-border workers. May involve:
- Double taxation agreements
- Different tax rates
- Special registration requirements
See SKAT website or consult tax advisor.
