# Glossary of Danish Terms

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Purpose**: Definitions and translations of Danish terms used in the documentation

## A

### A-kasse
**English**: Unemployment insurance fund  
**Pronunciation**: AH-kah-seh  
**Definition**: A voluntary unemployment insurance fund that provides dagpenge (unemployment benefits) to members who become unemployed. Membership requires monthly contributions.

### Andelsbolig
**English**: Cooperative housing association  
**Pronunciation**: AHN-dels-boh-lee  
**Definition**: A form of housing where residents own shares in a cooperative that owns the building, rather than owning their individual apartments outright.

### Arbejdsmarkedspension
**English**: Labor market pension  
**Pronunciation**: AR-bides-mar-ked-pen-shon  
**Definition**: Occupational pension scheme typically arranged through collective agreements between employers and unions.

### Årsopgørelse
**English**: Annual tax return statement  
**Pronunciation**: ORS-op-gur-el-seh  
**Definition**: The annual tax assessment from SKAT showing your income, deductions, and final tax calculation for the year.

## B

### Barsel
**English**: Parental leave  
**Pronunciation**: BAR-sel  
**Definition**: Maternity and paternity leave benefits provided to parents around the birth or adoption of a child.

### Bikort
**English**: Secondary tax card  
**Pronunciation**: BEE-kort  
**Definition**: Secondary tax card used for additional jobs or income sources beyond primary employment. Has no personal allowance and higher tax rate (~55%).

### Bundskat
**English**: Bottom tax  
**Pronunciation**: BOON-skat  
**Definition**: National income tax of approximately 12.09% applied to all taxable income after AM-bidrag deduction.

### Boligstøtte
**English**: Housing benefits  
**Pronunciation**: BOH-lee-stut-teh  
**Definition**: Government financial assistance to help eligible residents with housing costs, including rent and utilities.

### Borger.dk
**English**: Citizen portal  
**Pronunciation**: BOR-ger  
**Definition**: Official Danish government portal providing information and services for citizens and residents.

### Børnepenge
**English**: Child benefits  
**Pronunciation**: BUR-neh-peng-eh  
**Definition**: Financial support paid to families with children, including børne- og ungeydelse (child and youth allowance).

### Børne- og ungeydelse
**English**: Child and youth allowance  
**Pronunciation**: BUR-neh oh OON-eh-yew-del-seh  
**Definition**: Quarterly payments to families with children under 18, amount varies by child's age.

## C

### CPR-nummer
**English**: Civil registration number (Personal identification number)  
**Pronunciation**: SAY-PAY-AIR noo-mer  
**Definition**: A unique 10-digit identification number assigned to all residents of Denmark. Required for accessing most services including healthcare, banking, and employment. Format: DDMMYY-XXXX.

## D

### Dagpenge
**English**: Unemployment benefits  
**Pronunciation**: DAH-peng-eh  
**Definition**: Daily unemployment benefits paid to eligible members of an A-kasse who become unemployed.

### Dankort
**English**: Danish card  
**Pronunciation**: DAN-kort  
**Definition**: Denmark's national debit card system, widely accepted throughout the country for purchases and ATM withdrawals. Often combined with Visa as Visa/Dankort.

### Depositum
**English**: Security deposit  
**Pronunciation**: deh-poh-SEE-toom  
**Definition**: Refundable deposit paid to landlord when renting housing, typically equivalent to 3 months' rent.

### Digital Post
**English**: Digital mailbox  
**Pronunciation**: (English term used in Danish)  
**Definition**: Mandatory digital communication system for receiving official correspondence from government agencies and many private companies.

## F

### Folkepension
**English**: State pension  
**Pronunciation**: FOL-keh-pen-shon  
**Definition**: Public pension provided by the Danish state to residents who meet age and residency requirements.

### Forskerordningen
**English**: Researcher tax scheme  
**Pronunciation**: FOR-sker-ord-ning-en  
**Definition**: Special tax scheme for highly qualified researchers and key employees with a flat 27% tax rate for up to 84 months.

### Forskudsskat
**English**: Preliminary tax  
**Pronunciation**: FOR-skoos-skat  
**Definition**: Estimated taxes paid quarterly by self-employed individuals and those with income not subject to automatic withholding.

### Frikort
**English**: Free card / Tax-free card  
**Pronunciation**: FREE-kort  
**Definition**: Special tax card for low-income earners where no tax is withheld if annual income is below the personal allowance threshold.

### Fuld skattepligt
**English**: Full tax liability  
**Pronunciation**: FOOL skat-teh-plikt  
**Definition**: Tax status where you pay Danish tax on worldwide income, typically applies to residents with permanent home in Denmark or staying more than 6 months.

## I

### Hovedkort
**English**: Main tax card  
**Pronunciation**: HOH-ved-kort  
**Definition**: Primary tax card used for main employment, includes full personal allowance and lowest tax rate.

### Indboforsikring
**English**: Contents insurance / Household insurance  
**Pronunciation**: IND-boh-for-seek-ring  
**Definition**: Insurance covering personal belongings and liability in rental or owned housing. Often required by landlords.

### Indkomstskat
**English**: Income tax  
**Pronunciation**: IND-komst-skat  
**Definition**: Tax on income consisting of bottom tax (bundskat) and top tax (topskat) for higher earners.

### ICS
**English**: International Citizen Service  
**Pronunciation**: (English acronym)  
**Definition**: Service centers that assist international citizens with registration, permits, and integration in Denmark.

## K

### Kirkeskat
**English**: Church tax  
**Pronunciation**: KEER-keh-skat  
**Definition**: Optional tax (approximately 0.5-1.5%) that funds the Danish National Church (Folkekirken). Can opt out if not a church member.

### Kommuneskat
**English**: Municipal tax  
**Pronunciation**: ko-MOO-neh-skat  
**Definition**: Local tax that varies by municipality (typically 22-27%, average ~25%) and funds local services such as schools, childcare, and infrastructure.

### Kontanthjælp
**English**: Social assistance / Cash benefits  
**Pronunciation**: kon-TANT-yelp  
**Definition**: Financial support for individuals without other income sources who meet eligibility criteria.

## L

### Lejekontrakt
**English**: Rental contract / Lease agreement  
**Pronunciation**: LIE-eh-kon-trakt  
**Definition**: Legal contract between landlord and tenant specifying terms of rental, including rent amount, duration, and responsibilities.

### Lifeindenmark.dk
**English**: Life in Denmark portal  
**Pronunciation**: (English)  
**Definition**: Official English-language portal providing information for international citizens living in or moving to Denmark.

### Lønkonto
**English**: Salary account / Checking account  
**Pronunciation**: LURN-kon-toh  
**Definition**: Standard bank account used for receiving salary, paying bills, and daily transactions.

## M

### MitID
**English**: My ID  
**Pronunciation**: MIT-ID (meet-ID)  
**Definition**: Denmark's digital identity solution used to access government services, banking, and many private services. Replaced NemID in 2021.

### MobilePay
**English**: Mobile payment  
**Pronunciation**: (English term used in Danish)  
**Definition**: Denmark's most popular mobile payment app for person-to-person transfers and merchant payments. Requires a Danish bank account and phone number.

### Moms
**English**: VAT (Value Added Tax)  
**Pronunciation**: moms  
**Definition**: Sales tax applied to most goods and services in Denmark, standard rate is 25%.

## N

### NemKonto
**English**: Easy account  
**Pronunciation**: NEM-kon-toh  
**Definition**: Designated bank account registered with the government for receiving public payments such as tax refunds, benefits, and salary reimbursements.

### Newtodenmark.dk
**English**: New to Denmark portal  
**Pronunciation**: (English)  
**Definition**: Official portal specifically designed for newcomers to Denmark with information about arrival and settling in.

## O

### Opsparingskonto
**English**: Savings account  
**Pronunciation**: OP-spar-ings-kon-toh  
**Definition**: Bank account designed for saving money with higher interest rates but limited transaction capabilities.

## P

### Personfradrag
**English**: Personal allowance  
**Pronunciation**: per-SOHN-frah-drah  
**Definition**: Tax-free allowance automatically applied to reduce taxable income, approximately 49,700 DKK per year (2025).

### Personnummer
**English**: Personal number (same as CPR-nummer)  
**Pronunciation**: per-SOHN-noo-mer  
**Definition**: Another term for CPR-nummer, the civil registration number.

## S

### Restskat
**English**: Additional tax owed  
**Pronunciation**: REST-skat  
**Definition**: Additional tax that must be paid if you underpaid taxes during the year, typically due by August.

### SKAT
**English**: Danish Tax Authority  
**Pronunciation**: skat  
**Definition**: The Danish tax administration responsible for collecting taxes and managing tax-related matters.

### Skattekort
**English**: Tax card  
**Pronunciation**: SKAT-teh-kort  
**Definition**: Tax deduction card that tells employers how much tax to withhold from salary. Can be main card (hovedkort) or secondary card (bikort).

### SU
**English**: State Educational Support  
**Pronunciation**: ESS-OO  
**Definition**: Statens Uddannelsesstøtte - government grants and loans available to eligible students in Denmark.

### Sundhedskort
**English**: Health insurance card  
**Pronunciation**: SOON-heds-kort  
**Definition**: Yellow health insurance card showing CPR number, name, address, and assigned GP. Mailed 2-3 weeks after CPR registration.

## Abbreviations

### AM-bidrag
**English**: Labor market contribution  
**Pronunciation**: AH-EM bee-drah  
**Definition**: 8% tax on gross income that funds labor market programs and unemployment benefits.

### Begrænset skattepligt
**English**: Limited tax liability  
**Pronunciation**: beh-GREN-set skat-teh-plikt  
**Definition**: Tax status where you pay Danish tax only on Danish-source income, typically applies to non-residents working in Denmark.

### CVR
**English**: Central Business Register number  
**Pronunciation**: SAY-VAY-AIR  
**Definition**: Company registration number used to identify Danish businesses and organizations.

### EU
**English**: European Union  
**Definition**: Political and economic union of European member states. Citizens of EU/EEA countries and Switzerland have special rights in Denmark.

### EEA
**English**: European Economic Area  
**Definition**: Includes EU countries plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway.

### GP
**English**: General Practitioner (family doctor)  
**Definition**: Primary care physician that serves as first point of contact for healthcare. Registration with a GP is required in Denmark.

### Overskydende skat
**English**: Tax refund / Tax overpayment  
**Pronunciation**: OH-ver-skew-den-eh skat  
**Definition**: Tax refund received when you overpaid taxes during the year, automatically paid to NemKonto.

### TastSelv
**English**: Self-service (SKAT's online portal)  
**Pronunciation**: TAST-selv  
**Definition**: SKAT's online portal for managing tax matters, requires MitID login. Used for tax cards, annual assessments, and tax information.

### Topskat
**English**: Top tax  
**Pronunciation**: TOP-skat  
**Definition**: Additional 15% tax applied to income exceeding approximately 588,900 DKK per year (2025 threshold).
