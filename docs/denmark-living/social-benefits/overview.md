# Social Benefits - Overview

> **Last Updated**: 2025-01-15  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/  
> **Applies to**: Eligible residents

## Overview

Denmark provides comprehensive social benefits and support systems for residents who meet eligibility criteria. This section covers housing benefits, unemployment support, child benefits, pensions, parental leave, student support, social assistance, disability benefits, and elderly care services.

## Benefits Covered

### [Housing Benefits (Boligstøtte)](boligstotte.md)
Financial assistance for housing costs including rent and utilities. Eligibility based on income, family size, and housing expenses.

### [Unemployment Benefits (Dagpenge)](unemployment-benefits.md)
Support for unemployed workers who are members of an A-kasse (unemployment insurance fund). Requires work history and active job search.

### [Child Benefits (Børnepenge)](child-benefits.md)
Quarterly payments to families with children under 18. Automatic for residents with children.

### [Pension System](pension-system.md)
State pension (folkepension) and occupational pensions (arbejdsmarkedspension) for retirement.

### [Parental Leave (Barsel)](parental-leave.md)
Maternity and paternity leave benefits for parents around birth or adoption.

### [Student Support (SU)](student-support.md)
State educational grants and loans for eligible students in approved education programs.

### [Social Assistance (Kontanthjælp)](social-assistance.md)
Financial support for individuals without other income sources who meet eligibility criteria.

### [Disability Benefits](disability-benefits.md)
Support services and financial assistance for people with disabilities.

### [Elderly Care](elderly-care.md)
Services and support for senior citizens including home care and nursing homes.

## Eligibility Overview

Most benefits require:
- **CPR number**: Civil registration in Denmark
- **Residency**: Minimum residency period (varies by benefit)
- **Work history**: For some benefits like dagpenge
- **Income limits**: For means-tested benefits
- **Active requirements**: Job search, education participation, etc.

## Common Questions

### How do I know which benefits I'm eligible for?

Check each benefit's specific eligibility criteria. Key factors:
- Residency duration in Denmark
- Employment history
- Current income and assets
- Family situation
- Age

### Do I need to apply for child benefits?

No, børnepenge is automatic once you have a CPR number and registered children. Payments start automatically.

### Can I receive multiple benefits at once?

Some benefits can be combined, others cannot. For example:
- ✓ Child benefits + employment income
- ✓ Housing benefits + employment income (if income is low enough)
- ✗ Unemployment benefits + social assistance (choose one)

### How long do I need to live in Denmark to qualify?

Varies by benefit:
- **Child benefits**: Immediate with CPR
- **Housing benefits**: Immediate with CPR
- **Unemployment benefits**: Requires A-kasse membership (typically 1 year)
- **State pension**: Requires 3 years residency between ages 15-65
- **SU**: Varies by citizenship and residency

## Application Process

Most benefits are applied for through:
- **Udbetaling Danmark**: Housing benefits, child benefits, pensions
- **A-kasse**: Unemployment benefits (through your specific fund)
- **SU.dk**: Student support
- **Local municipality**: Social assistance, disability benefits
