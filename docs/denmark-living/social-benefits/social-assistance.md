# Social Assistance (Kontanthjælp)

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/arbejde-dagpenge-og-sygedagpenge/kontanthjaelp  
> **Applies to**: Residents Without Other Income Sources

## Overview

**Kontanthjælp** (social assistance) is Denmark's **safety net** benefit providing basic financial support to residents who cannot support themselves through work, unemployment benefits, or other income sources. It serves as the **last resort** assistance while helping recipients move toward **self-sufficiency** through employment or education.

## Key Information

- **Who is eligible**: Residents without other income sources or benefits
- **When to apply**: When facing financial hardship with no other options
- **Prerequisites**: Residency requirements, asset limits, job search obligations
- **Processing time**: 2-4 weeks for initial application processing

## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Eligibility

**To qualify for kontanthjælp**:
- **Legal residence** in Denmark
- **Age 18 or older** (or 25+ for full rates in some cases)
- **Unable to support** yourself through work
- **Not eligible** for unemployment benefits or other support
- **Available for work** and actively job searching

### Residency Requirements

**Residence criteria**:
- **Legal right** to stay in Denmark
- **Registered address** in Danish municipality
- **Continuous residence** for specified periods (varies by situation)
- **Integration requirements** may apply for some groups

### Asset Limits

**Financial thresholds**:
- **Limited savings** and assets allowed
- **Home ownership** may affect eligibility
- **Vehicle value** restrictions
- **Must use own resources** before receiving assistance

## Benefit Amounts

### Standard Rates (2025)

**Monthly kontanthjælp amounts**:
- **Adults 30+**: Approximately 15,000 DKK per month
- **Adults 25-29**: Approximately 12,500 DKK per month (reduced rate)
- **Adults under 25**: Approximately 7,500 DKK per month (education rate)
- **Couples**: Reduced individual amounts when both receive assistance

### Additional Support

**Supplementary assistance**:
- **Housing costs** (boligstøtte) separate application
- **Childcare support** for working parents
- **Transportation** assistance for job search
- **Special circumstances** additional support

### Reductions and Sanctions

**Benefit may be reduced for**:
- **Failure to meet** job search requirements
- **Refusing suitable** work or training offers
- **Not attending** mandatory meetings or programs
- **Violation of** program rules and obligations

## Application Process

### Initial Application

1. **Contact Municipality**
   - Visit local **jobcenter** or social services office
   - Bring all required documentation
   - Complete detailed application forms
   - Attend initial assessment meeting

2. **Required Documents**
   - **CPR card** and identification
   - **Bank statements** for past 3 months
   - **Employment history** and termination documentation
   - **Housing costs** documentation (rent, utilities)
   - **Asset declarations** (property, vehicles, investments)

3. **Assessment Process**
   - **Financial evaluation** of need and eligibility
   - **Work capacity** assessment
   - **Integration plan** development
   - **Benefit calculation** and approval

### Ongoing Obligations

**Recipients must**:
- **Report changes** in circumstances immediately
- **Attend regular** meetings with caseworker
- **Participate in** job search and training programs
- **Accept suitable** work offers
- **Complete monthly** reporting requirements

## Work and Integration Requirements

### Job Search Obligations

**Active job seeking**:
- **Apply for jobs** as directed by jobcenter
- **Attend job interviews** when arranged
- **Participate in** networking and job search activities
- **Document job search** efforts regularly

### Training and Education Programs

**Skill development**:
- **Mandatory participation** in relevant training
- **Danish language** courses for non-native speakers
- **Vocational training** programs
- **Work experience** placements (praktik)

### Integration Plans

**Individualized support**:
- **Personal action plan** with specific goals
- **Regular progress** reviews and adjustments
- **Barrier identification** and support services
- **Timeline for** achieving self-sufficiency

## Special Categories

### Young Adults (Under 25)

**Reduced benefits** with focus on education:
- **Lower payment rates** to encourage education/training
- **Education requirement** for full benefits
- **Special youth programs** and support
- **Parental support** expectations in some cases

### Families with Children

**Enhanced support**:
- **Additional allowances** for children
- **Childcare support** to enable work/training
- **Family-specific** integration programs
- **Child welfare** considerations in planning

### Refugees and Immigrants

**Integration focus**:
- **Integration allowance** (integrationsydelse) for some groups
- **Language learning** requirements
- **Cultural orientation** programs
- **Specialized support** services

### People with Health Issues

**Health-related support**:
- **Medical assessments** for work capacity
- **Rehabilitation programs** and support
- **Flexible requirements** based on health status
- **Coordination with** healthcare services

## Rights and Responsibilities

### Recipient Rights

**Your rights include**:
- **Fair treatment** and respect
- **Clear information** about requirements and benefits
- **Appeal procedures** for decisions
- **Privacy protection** for personal information
- **Interpreter services** if needed

### Responsibilities

**Recipients must**:
- **Provide accurate** information
- **Comply with** program requirements
- **Participate actively** in integration activities
- **Report changes** in circumstances
- **Respect staff** and program rules

## Appeals and Complaints

### Appeal Process

**If you disagree** with decisions:
1. **Request reconsideration** from municipality
2. **Submit formal appeal** to Ankestyrelsen (Appeals Board)
3. **Provide additional** documentation if available
4. **Await decision** (can take several months)

### Legal Support

**Getting help**:
- **Free legal aid** for appeals
- **Advocacy organizations** and support groups
- **Trade union** assistance (if member)
- **Municipal ombudsman** for procedural complaints

## Transition to Work

### Employment Support

**Job placement assistance**:
- **Job matching** services
- **Interview preparation** and coaching
- **Work clothing** and equipment support
- **Transportation** assistance for work

### Wage Subsidies

**Employer incentives**:
- **Løntilskud** (wage subsidies) for employers
- **Gradual transition** from benefits to full employment
- **Job training** on-the-job programs
- **Mentorship** and support services

### Education Pathways

**Skill development**:
- **Vocational training** with continued support
- **Adult education** programs
- **University preparation** courses
- **Career counseling** and guidance
