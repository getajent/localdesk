# Pension System

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/pension-og-efterloen  
> **Applies to**: All Workers and Residents

## Overview

Denmark's **pensionssystem** (pension system) provides retirement income through a **three-pillar structure**: public pension (**folkepension**), occupational pension (**arbejdsmarkedspension**), and private pension savings. The system ensures **basic income security** for all residents while encouraging additional savings for comfortable retirement.

## Key Information

- **Who is covered**: All residents and workers in Denmark
- **When benefits start**: Folkepension at age 67 (gradually increasing)
- **Prerequisites**: Residency requirements vary by pension type
- **Processing time**: Applications processed 3-6 months before retirement age

## Three-Pillar Pension Structure

### First Pillar: Public Pension (Folkepension)

**Universal basic pension** for all residents:
- **Tax-funded** through general government revenue
- **Residence-based** eligibility (not work-based)
- **Means-tested** with income and asset limits
- **Guaranteed minimum** income in retirement

### Second Pillar: Occupational Pension (Arbejdsmarkedspension)

**Workplace pension schemes**:
- **Mandatory contributions** for most employees
- **Employer and employee** contributions
- **Collective agreements** determine contribution rates
- **Professional management** of pension funds

### Third Pillar: Private Pension Savings

**Individual retirement savings**:
- **Voluntary contributions** with tax advantages
- **Personal control** over investment choices
- **Flexible withdrawal** options
- **Supplementary income** for retirement

## Public Pension (Folkepension)

### Eligibility Requirements

**Basic requirements**:
- **Age 67** (retirement age increasing gradually to 68 by 2030)
- **40 years of residence** in Denmark for full pension
- **Proportional pension** for shorter residence periods
- **Legal residence** status required

### Pension Components

**Basic amount** (grundbeløb):
- **Fixed monthly amount** for all eligible residents
- **Not means-tested** against other pension income
- **Adjusted annually** for inflation
- **Currently approximately** 6,500 DKK per month (2025)

**Pension supplement** (pensionstillæg):
- **Income-tested** additional amount
- **Reduced based on** other pension income
- **Asset testing** for significant wealth
- **Maximum supplement** approximately 7,000 DKK per month

### Income Testing

**Pension reduction** based on:
- **Occupational pension** income above threshold
- **Private pension** withdrawals
- **Employment income** (limited work allowed)
- **Capital income** from investments

**Thresholds and reductions**:
- **Income threshold**: Approximately 350,000 DKK annually
- **Reduction rate**: 30% of income above threshold
- **Asset threshold**: 905,100 DKK for singles, 1,810,200 DKK for couples
- **Asset reduction**: 30% of assets above threshold

## Occupational Pension (Arbejdsmarkedspension)

### Mandatory Coverage

**ATP (Arbejdsmarkedets Tillægspension)**:
- **Compulsory** for all employees and benefit recipients
- **Fixed contributions** regardless of income level
- **Guaranteed benefits** based on contribution years
- **Lifetime annuity** payments in retirement

### Collective Agreement Pensions

**Industry-specific schemes**:
- **Negotiated contribution rates** (typically 12-18% of salary)
- **Employer contributions** (usually 2/3 of total)
- **Employee contributions** (usually 1/3 of total)
- **Professional fund management**

### Contribution Rates by Sector

**Typical contribution rates**:
- **Public sector**: 15-18% of salary
- **Private sector**: 12-15% of salary
- **Academic professions**: 16-18% of salary
- **Manual workers**: 12-14% of salary

### Investment Options

**Pension fund choices**:
- **Conservative portfolios** with guaranteed returns
- **Balanced portfolios** with moderate risk
- **Growth portfolios** with higher risk/return
- **Lifecycle funds** adjusting risk with age

## Private Pension Savings

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts

**Ratepension** (Annuity pension):
- **Tax deduction** on contributions
- **Tax-free growth** during accumulation
- **Taxable income** when withdrawn as annuity
- **Mandatory annuity** conversion at retirement

**Kapitalpension** (Capital pension):
- **No tax deduction** on contributions
- **Tax-free growth** during accumulation
- **Reduced tax rate** (15.3%) on lump sum withdrawal
- **Flexible withdrawal** options

**Aldersopsparing** (Retirement savings):
- **No tax deduction** on contributions
- **Tax-free growth** and withdrawal
- **Annual contribution limit** (approximately 30,000 DKK)
- **Withdrawal from age 60**

### Investment Choices

**Available options**:
- **Bank deposits** with guaranteed returns
- **Insurance products** with guarantees
- **Mutual funds** and ETFs
- **Individual stocks** and bonds

## Pension Application Process

### Folkepension Application

**Application timeline**:
1. **Apply 3-6 months** before reaching retirement age
2. **Submit application** through borger.dk or local municipality
3. **Provide documentation** of residence history and income
4. **Await processing** and pension calculation
5. **Receive decision** and payment start date

**Required documents**:
- **CPR certificate** and residence documentation
- **Income statements** from all sources
- **Asset declarations** including property and investments
- **Foreign pension** information (if applicable)

### Occupational Pension Claims

**Claiming process**:
- **Contact pension fund** directly
- **Submit retirement** notification to employer
- **Choose payout options** (annuity, lump sum, combination)
- **Coordinate timing** with other pension sources

## Early Retirement Options

### Efterløn (Early Retirement Pay)

**Voluntary early retirement**:
- **Available from age 62** (increasing to 64 by 2030)
- **Requires unemployment insurance** membership for 30 years
- **Reduced benefits** compared to full pension
- **Bridge to folkepension** at age 67

### Seniorpension (Senior Pension)

**For reduced work capacity**:
- **Available from age 60**
- **Medical assessment** required
- **Permanent work reduction** of at least 50%
- **Bridge benefit** until folkepension age

### Disability Pension

**For permanent disability**:
- **No age requirement**
- **Medical documentation** of permanent work incapacity
- **Means-tested** like folkepension
- **Converts to folkepension** at retirement age

## International Considerations

### EU/EEA Coordination

**Cross-border pensions**:
- **Pension rights** portable within EU/EEA
- **Coordination of benefits** to avoid double taxation
- **Proportional benefits** based on work/residence periods
- **Application through** home country authorities

### Bilateral Agreements

**Non-EU countries** with agreements:
- **United States**: Social Security totalization
- **Canada**: Pension coordination
- **Australia**: Superannuation agreements
- **Other countries**: Various bilateral treaties

### Tax Treaties

**Avoiding double taxation**:
- **Tax treaty benefits** for pension income
- **Foreign tax credits** for taxes paid abroad
- **Residence-based taxation** rules
- **Professional advice** recommended for complex cases

## Pension Planning Strategies

### Maximizing Benefits

**Optimization strategies**:
- **Maximize occupational** pension contributions
- **Utilize tax-advantaged** private savings
- **Consider timing** of pension claims
- **Coordinate multiple** pension sources

### Common Mistakes

**Avoid these pitfalls**:
- **Insufficient private savings** beyond mandatory pensions
- **Poor investment choices** in pension accounts
- **Inadequate planning** for tax implications
- **Ignoring inflation** impact on fixed benefits
