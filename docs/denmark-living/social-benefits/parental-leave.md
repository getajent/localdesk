# Parental Leave and Benefits

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/familie-og-boern/barsel-og-foraeldre  
> **Applies to**: All Parents and Expecting Parents

## Overview

Denmark's **barsel og forældreorlov** (parental leave and benefits) system provides comprehensive support for new parents through **paid leave**, **job protection**, and **flexible arrangements**. The system recognizes both **maternity** and **paternity** rights while encouraging **shared parenting** responsibilities.

## Key Information

- **Who is eligible**: All employed parents and some self-employed
- **When to apply**: Before birth or as early as possible after birth
- **Prerequisites**: Employment or unemployment benefits, residence in Denmark
- **Processing time**: 2-4 weeks for benefit approval

## Types of Parental Leave

### Maternity Leave (Barselsorlov)

**For birth mothers**:
- **4 weeks before** expected due date (optional)
- **14 weeks after** birth (mandatory)
- **Total 18 weeks** of maternity-specific leave
- **Medical protection** and job security

### Paternity Leave (Fædreorlov)

**For fathers and co-mothers**:
- **2 weeks immediately** after birth (reserved)
- **Cannot be transferred** to birth mother
- **Encourages early** bonding and support
- **Full pay** during leave period

### Parental Leave (Forældreorlov)

**Shared between parents**:
- **32 weeks total** to be divided between parents
- **Flexible distribution** according to family needs
- **Can be taken** until child turns 1 year old
- **Some weeks** can be extended until child turns 2

## Leave Entitlements and Duration

### Total Leave Available

**Standard entitlements**:
- **Maternity leave**: 4 weeks before + 14 weeks after birth
- **Paternity leave**: 2 weeks reserved for non-birth parent
- **Shared parental leave**: 32 weeks to divide
- **Total potential**: Up to 52 weeks of paid leave

### Flexible Arrangements

**Leave can be taken**:
- **Consecutively** by one parent
- **Simultaneously** by both parents (reducing total weeks)
- **In blocks** with return to work between periods
- **Part-time** combined with reduced working hours

### Extension Options

**Extending leave**:
- **Up to 14 weeks** can be extended until child turns 2
- **Reduced benefits** during extended period
- **Employer agreement** required for job protection
- **Maintains some** income support

## Parental Benefits (Barselsdagpenge)

### Benefit Calculation

**Payment amounts**:
- **Maximum weekly benefit**: Approximately 4,465 DKK (2025)
- **Based on previous income** up to maximum
- **Minimum benefit**: For those with limited work history
- **Tax-free** benefit payments

### Eligibility Requirements

**To qualify for benefits**:
- **Employment** for at least 160 hours in 4 weeks before leave
- **Unemployment benefits** recipient
- **Self-employed** with proper documentation
- **Residence** in Denmark during leave

### Application Process

1. **Notify employer** of intended leave dates
2. **Apply online** through borger.dk or Udbetaling Danmark
3. **Provide documentation** of employment and pregnancy/birth
4. **Submit medical certificates** if required
5. **Receive benefit** payments during leave

## Employer Obligations

### Job Protection

**Employee rights**:
- **Guaranteed return** to same or equivalent position
- **Continued employment** benefits during leave
- **No discrimination** based on pregnancy or parental leave
- **Reasonable accommodations** for pregnant employees

### Salary Supplements

**Enhanced benefits**:
- Many **collective agreements** provide full salary during leave
- **Employer supplements** to government benefits
- **Extended leave** periods beyond legal minimum
- **Gradual return** to work arrangements

### Workplace Flexibility

**Supportive measures**:
- **Flexible working** hours for new parents
- **Breastfeeding breaks** and facilities
- **Reduced travel** requirements
- **Family-friendly** policies and culture

## Special Circumstances

### Multiple Births

**Twins, triplets, etc.**:
- **Additional 46 weeks** of parental leave for multiple births
- **Extended benefits** period
- **Increased support** for complex needs
- **Flexible arrangements** for care coordination

### Premature Birth

**Early delivery**:
- **Extended maternity** leave if child born before 37 weeks
- **Additional weeks** equal to prematurity period
- **Medical support** and specialized care
- **Flexible return** to work timing

### Adoption

**Adoptive parents**:
- **Same leave entitlements** as biological parents
- **24 weeks** to be shared between adoptive parents
- **Preparation leave** for adoption process
- **International adoption** considerations

### Single Parents

**Solo parenting**:
- **Full leave entitlement** available to single parent
- **Extended support** services
- **Childcare assistance** programs
- **Financial counseling** and support

## Coordination with Other Benefits

### Child Benefits

**Concurrent benefits**:
- **Børnepenge** (child allowance) continues during leave
- **Childcare subsidies** available after return to work
- **Healthcare benefits** maintained
- **Housing benefits** may be affected by income changes

### Unemployment Benefits

**Transition considerations**:
- **Return to unemployment** benefits if job ends during leave
- **Maintaining eligibility** for future benefits
- **Job search requirements** after leave ends
- **Retraining opportunities** for career changes

## International Considerations

### EU/EEA Coordination

**Cross-border situations**:
- **Work in one country**, live in another
- **Coordination of benefits** to avoid gaps or overlaps
- **Portable rights** within EU/EEA
- **Application procedures** for cross-border cases

### Non-EU Citizens

**Residence requirements**:
- **Legal residence** status required
- **Work permit** implications for benefits
- **Documentation requirements** may be more extensive
- **Integration support** services available

## Planning Your Parental Leave

### Before Birth

**Preparation steps**:
- **Discuss plans** with employer early
- **Review collective agreement** for enhanced benefits
- **Plan financial** impact and budgeting
- **Coordinate with partner** for optimal arrangement

### During Pregnancy

**Key actions**:
- **Submit leave notification** to employer
- **Apply for benefits** in advance
- **Arrange temporary** work coverage
- **Prepare for** gradual return to work

### After Birth

**Important tasks**:
- **Register child** for CPR number
- **Apply for child benefits** and allowances
- **Update insurance** and benefit information
- **Plan childcare** for return to work

## Return to Work

### Gradual Return

**Flexible options**:
- **Part-time return** with reduced hours
- **Job sharing** arrangements
- **Remote work** opportunities
- **Flexible scheduling** for family needs

### Childcare Arrangements

**Care options**:
- **Municipal childcare** (vuggestue, børnehave)
- **Private childcare** providers
- **Family daycare** (dagpleje)
- **Relative care** arrangements

### Workplace Support

**Continuing support**:
- **Breastfeeding facilities** and break time
- **Family emergency** leave policies
- **Career development** opportunities
- **Work-life balance** initiatives

## Common Questions

### Can both parents take leave simultaneously?

Yes, but this **reduces the total** number of weeks available. When both parents are on leave at the same time, it counts as double weeks against your total entitlement.

### What if I want to extend my leave?

You can **extend up to 14 weeks** until your child turns 2, but with **reduced benefits**. Your employer must agree to extended unpaid leave for job protection.

### Can I work part-time during parental leave?

Yes, you can **combine part-time work** with partial parental benefits, allowing for a gradual transition back to full-time work.

### What happens if I lose my job during leave?

You may be eligible for **unemployment benefits** instead of parental benefits. Contact your unemployment insurance fund (A-kasse) immediately.
