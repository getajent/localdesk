# Elderly Care Services

> **Last Updated**: 2025-02-03  
> **Official Source**: https://borger.dk/familie-og-boern/aeldreomraadet  
> **Applies to**: Seniors and Their Families

## Overview

Denmark's **ældreomsorg** (elderly care system) provides comprehensive support services for **seniors** to maintain **independence**, **dignity**, and **quality of life** as they age. The system emphasizes **aging in place** with community-based services while providing institutional care when needed.

## Key Information

- **Who is eligible**: Residents aged 65+ or those with age-related care needs
- **When to apply**: When assistance is needed for daily activities
- **Prerequisites**: Assessment of care needs, residency in municipality
- **Processing time**: 2-4 weeks for care assessment and service arrangement

## Types of Elderly Care Services

### Home Care Services (Hjemmepleje)

**Support for living at home**:
- **Personal care** assistance (bathing, dressing, medication)
- **Household tasks** (cleaning, shopping, meal preparation)
- **Healthcare services** (nursing care, wound care)
- **Social support** and companionship visits

### Day Centers (Dagcentre)

**Community-based activities**:
- **Social interaction** and activities
- **Meals and** nutrition support
- **Health monitoring** and basic care
- **Transportation** to and from center

### Respite Care (Aflastning)

**Temporary care** for family caregivers:
- **Short-term stays** in care facilities
- **Day respite** services
- **Emergency backup** care
- **Planned breaks** for family caregivers

### Residential Care

**When home care insufficient**:
- **Assisted living** facilities (plejeboliger)
- **Nursing homes** (plejehjem)
- **Specialized dementia** care units
- **Rehabilitation** facilities

## Assessment and Eligibility

### Care Needs Assessment

**Evaluation process**:
- **Comprehensive assessment** by municipal care team
- **Physical and cognitive** capacity evaluation
- **Social circumstances** and support network review
- **Safety and** independence considerations

### Assessment Criteria

**Factors considered**:
- **Activities of daily living** (ADL) capacity
- **Instrumental activities** (IADL) abilities
- **Cognitive function** and memory
- **Social isolation** and support needs
- **Health conditions** and medical needs

### Reassessment

**Ongoing evaluation**:
- **Regular reviews** of care needs (typically annually)
- **Changes in condition** trigger new assessments
- **Service adjustments** based on evolving needs
- **Family input** in care planning

## Housing Options for Seniors

### Aging in Place

**Staying at home**:
- **Home modifications** for accessibility
- **Safety equipment** and grab bars
- **Ramp installation** and accessibility improvements
- **Technology integration** for independence

### Senior Housing (Ældreboliger)

**Age-appropriate housing**:
- **Accessible apartments** designed for seniors
- **Community facilities** and social spaces
- **On-site services** and support
- **Emergency response** systems

### Assisted Living (Plejeboliger)

**Supported independent living**:
- **Private apartments** with care services available
- **24/7 staff** presence
- **Meals and** housekeeping services
- **Social activities** and programs

### Nursing Homes (Plejehjem)

**Comprehensive care facilities**:
- **24/7 nursing** care
- **Medical services** on-site
- **Specialized care** for dementia and complex needs
- **End-of-life** care and support

### Palliative and End-of-Life Care

## Costs and Financial Support

### Service Costs

**Fee structure**:
- **Income-based fees** for most services
- **Maximum monthly** payment caps
- **Free services** for those with limited income
- **Additional costs** for extra services or amenities

### Typical Costs (2025)

**Monthly fees**:
- **Home care**: 0-3,500 DKK based on income
- **Day center**: 100-500 DKK per day
- **Assisted living**: 1,500-4,000 DKK plus rent
- **Nursing home**: 2,000-5,000 DKK plus accommodation

### Financial Assistance

**Support available**:
- **Housing benefits** (boligstøtte) for seniors
- **Pension supplements** for care costs
- **Municipal subsidies** for low-income seniors
- **Equipment loans** and grants
